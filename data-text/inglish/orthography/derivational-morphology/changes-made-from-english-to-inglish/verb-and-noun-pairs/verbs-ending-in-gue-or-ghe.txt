Nouns and Verbs in Inglish: -ghe and -gue 

In Inglish, words ending in -ghe and -gue follow distinct patterns to differentiate between their noun and verb forms. 
Verb forms retain the -gue or -ghe endings, with the present participle marked by -guing for -gue verbs and -ghing for -ghe verbs. 
Noun forms drop the verb ending and add -s for the plural.

Vowel Quality Based on Endings:
    -gue: Typically indicates a long vowel or diphthong like /ɔ/, /æ/, /eɪ/, or /i/.
    -ghe: Typically indicates a short vowel like /ɑ/, /ɛ/, or /ɪ/.
