IPA /ɔ/ — "THOUGHT" VOWEL

SOUND OVERVIEW
- IPA symbol: /ɔ/
- Description: Mid-back rounded vowel
- Audio equivalent: as in "thought", "caught", "lawn"
- Frequency: Moderately common in Inglish, several spelling patterns

SPELLING PATTERNS
- Primary: AU (most common), -OH (past tenses)
- Stress-marked: Ò (clarification)
- Secondary: -AUH (rare)

DETAILED RULES

1. AU = /ɔ/ (Most Common)
   - Basic words: Australia → Austrâlha, autumn → automn
   - Automatic: automatic → automatic
   - Lawn family: lawn → laun, dawn → daun, pawn → paun
   - Coffee: coffee → caufie

2. Verb/Noun Distinction with AU
   - Verbs use -AUHE: to saw → ta sauhe, to draw → ta drauhe
   - Nouns use -AƜ: the saw → þe saɯ, the draw → þe draɯ

3. -OH = /ɔ/ (Irregular Past Tenses)
   - Bought: bought → boht
   - Caught: caught → coht
   - Fought: fought → foht
   - Sought: sought → soht
   - Thought: thought → þoht

4. Ò = /ɔ/ (Stressed)
   - Cost: cost → còst
   - Frost: frost → fròst
   - Cough: cough → còff

EXAMPLES BY CATEGORY
- Basic AU: Austrâlha, automn, automatic, laun, daun, paun, caufie
- Verb/noun pairs: sauhe/saɯ, drauhe/draɯ
- OH past tenses: boht, coht, foht, soht, þoht
- Stressed Ò: còst, fròst, còff

CROSS-REFERENCES
