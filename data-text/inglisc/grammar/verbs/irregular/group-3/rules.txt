GROUP 3 IRREGULAR VERBS: CONJUGATION PATTERNS

1. Strong Verb Ablaut Patterns

Most of these verbs show ablaut (vowel alternation) patterns across their forms:

i-a-u pattern:
begin: to biguin -s begane begone biguining
swim: to sɯimme sɯims sɯame sɯom sɯimming

i-â-o pattern:
ring: to ringue -s râng rong ringuing
sing: to singue -s sâng song singuing
spring: to springue -s sprâng sprong springuing
sling: to slingue -s slâng slong slinguing
string: to stringue -s strâng strong stringuing
stink: to stinke -s stânk stonk stinking
drink: to drinke -s drânk dronk drinking
fling: to flingue -s flâng flong flinguing

oɯ-ou-oɯn pattern:
  blow: to bloɯe -s blou bloɯn bloɯing
  grow: to groɯe -s grou groɯn groɯing
  know: to noɯe -s nou noɯn noɯing
  throw: to þroɯe -s þrou þroɯn þroɯing

î-o-i pattern:
  drive: to drîve -s drove drivan -ing
  ride: to rîde -s rode ridden -ing
  arise: to arîse -s arose arisan -ing
  rise: to rîse -s rose risan -ing
  write: to ɯrîte -s ɯrote ɯritten -ing
  stride: to strîde -s strode stridden -ing

î-i-i pattern:
  bite: to bîte -s bit bitten -ing
  hide: to hîde -s hid hidden -ing

air-ore-ône pattern:
  bear: to baire -s bore bône -ing
  swear: to sɯaire -s sɯore sɯône -ing
  tear: to taire -s tore tône -ing
  wear: to ɯaire -s ɯore ɯône -ing

ie-o-o pattern:
  freeze: to friese -s frose frosen -ing
  speak: to spieke -s spoke spoken -ing
  weave: to ɯieve -s ɯove ɯoven -ing

a-e-a pattern:
  fall: to falle -s fell fallen -ing

auh-ou-ò pattern:
  draw: to drauhe -s drou dròn drauhing

i-â-i pattern:
  eat: to ite -s âte iten -ing

ue-ó-o pattern:
  get: to guet -s gót gotten -ing

ui-â-ui pattern:
  forgive: to forguive -s forgâve forguiven -ing
  give: to guive -s gâve guiven -ing

â-u-â pattern:
  shake: to seâke -s seuke seâken -ing
  take: to tâke -s tuke tâken -ing

ia-o-o pattern:
  steal: to stiale -s stole stolen -ing

î-u pattern:
  strike: to strîke -s struck -ing

é/â-o-o pattern:
  break: to bréke -s broke broken -ing
  wake: to ɯâke -s ɯoke ɯoken -ing

yh-ou-o pattern:
  fly: to flyhe -s flou flone -ing


2. Suppletive Verbs

Some verbs use completely different stems for different forms:
  be: to bie: am, is, are - ɯas, ɯere - bìn - bying
  go: to goɯe -s ɯent gòn -ing


3. Compound Verbs

Compounds follow the pattern of their base verb:
- overthrow → to overþroɯe -s overþrou overþroɯn -ing
- undergo → to ondregoɯe -s ondreɯent ondregòn -ing
- withdraw → to ɯiþdrauhe -s ɯiþdrou ɯiþdròn -ing
