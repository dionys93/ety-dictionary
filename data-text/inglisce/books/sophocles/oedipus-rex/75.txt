[OEDIPUS]

I uill not have come to chille my fáþre,
and men uill not sihe in mi þe husband
[of] þe uôman hu gâve [birth] to mi.
Nôe I am abàndoned by þe [gods,]
þe sone [of] [a] corropted maþre,
[conceiving] c̃ildren uiþ þe uôman
hu gâve mi my one mïserable lîfe.
[If] þere am som horrific [suffering]
uirse þan all þe [rest,] [then] it tou belòngs
in þe fâte [of] [Oedipus.]


CÓROCE [LEADER]

I dou not believe
uate you did to yourselfe am for þe best.
[Better] to bie daid þan alîve and blînd.


[OEDIPUS]

Did telle mi uate I've don am not þe best.
And from nôe on spaire mi your advîse.
[If] I can sihe, I did gnoe hôe my eais
can lôche at my one fáþre uene I come
to [Hades] or at my rac̃id maþre.
Against þose tue I have comitted acs
[so] vîel þat ívan [if] I [hanged] myselfe
þat uill not bie sofiçent ponișement.
[Perhaps] you þinche þe sîhte [of] my one c̃ildren
maie guive mi joie? [No!]! Lôche hôe þey is [born!]
Ћey can nevre bringue delîte to eais [of] mîne.
[Nor] can þe citie or its [massive] ualls,
or þe sâcrid images [of] its [gods.]
I am þe [most] [abhorred] [of] men, I,
þe fînest man [of] altogaiþre þose [bred] in [Thebes,]
I have condemned myselfe, telling [everyone]
þey have to banișe for [impiety]
þe man þe [gods] have nôe exposed
as sacriligeus—[a] sone [of] [Laius,] tou.
Uiþ soc̃ [polluting] staîns upon mi,
can I sete eais on you and hólde your gâse?
[No.]. And [if] I can [somehow] bloche my iars
