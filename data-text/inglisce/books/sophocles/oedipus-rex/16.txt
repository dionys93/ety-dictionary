OEDIPUS

Teiresias,
you hu onderstande all þings—uate cane bie tauht
and uate canenot bie spócan [of], uate goes on
in hevon and hiere on þe Erþ—you gnoe,
[although] you canenot sihe, hôe [sick] aur stâte is.
And [so] uie fînde in you alone, great siheur,
aur sielde and [saviour]. For Phoebus Apollo,
in caisse you have not hird þe nous, has sent os
anoþre ansere to aur quaistion: þe [only] cure
for þis infecting [pestilence] is to fînde
þe men hu mourdred Laius and chille þem
or else expelle þem from þis [land] as [exiles].
[So] dou not uiþhólde from os your prophecis
from voices [of] þe birds or by som oþre míns.
Sâve þis citie and yourselfe. Rèscue mi.
Delivre os from all [pollution] by þe daid.
Uie are in your [hands]. For [a] [mortal] man
þe fîn [labour] hie cane dou is helpe
uiþ all his pôure oþre húman byings.


TEIRESIAS

[Alas], [alas]! Hôe [dreadful] it cane bie
to have uisdom uene it brings no bènefit
to þe man posessing it. Ћis I gnou,
but it had [slipped] my mŷnde. Oþreuîs,
I oûd not have journied hiere.


OEDIPUS

Uate is ròng? You have com, but sime [distressed].
