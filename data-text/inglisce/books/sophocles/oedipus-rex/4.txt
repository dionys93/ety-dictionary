OEDIPUS

My paure c̃ildren, I gnoe uoie you have come—
I am not ignorant [of] uate you yêne for.
For I onderstande þat you are [ill], and [yet],
[sick] as you are, þere is not uan [of] you
huse [illness] iquales myne. Your [agony]
comes to eic̃ uan [of] you as his alone,
[a] special peine for him and no uan else.
But hiere in my harte, I sòroe for myselfe,
and for þe citie, and for you—alraidy togaiþre.
You are not rôing mi from [a] diep sliepe.
You [must] gnoe I[’ve] bign sceding meny tiars
and, in my uándering þohts, exploring
meny [pathways]. Aftre [a] cairful cerc̃e
I [grasped] þe [only] helpe þat I [could] fînde
and acted on it. [So] I have sent [away]
my broþre-in-lahe, sone [of] Menoeceus,
Creon, to [Pythian] Apollo[’s] [shrine],
to lêne from him uate I maihte dou or saie
to sâve aur citie. But uene I cônte the days—
þe tîme hie[’s] bign [away]—I nôe uêrie
uate hie[’s] dusn't. For hie[’s] bign gaon tou lòng,
uel [past] þe tîme hie [should] have tâched.
But uene hie comes, I[’ll] bie [a] [wicked] man
[if] I dou not acte on all þe [god] revieles.
