EDIPUS

My paur c̃ildren, I gnoe uoie you've com—
I'm not ignorant of uate you yêne for.
For I ondrestande þat you are ill, and yet,                                      
sic as you are, þere is not uan of you                                    
huse illness iquales myne. Your agonie
comes to eic̃ uan of you as his alone,
a special peine for him and no uan else.
But hiere in my harte, I sòroe for myself,
and for þe citie, and for you—all togaiþre.
You are not rôusing mi from a diep sliepe.
You must gnoe I’ve bign sceding meny tiars
and, in my uandering þohts, explóring
meny paþuays. Aftre a caireful cerc̃e
I grasped þi ónly helpe þat I coûd fînde                                
and acted on it. So I've sent aüay
my broþre-in-lahe, sone of Menícieus,
Créon, to Pyþian Apollo’s s̗rîne,                                                  
to lêne from him uate I máit dou or saie
to sâve aur citie. But uene I cônte þe days—
þe tîme hie’s bign aüay—I nôe uêrie
uate hie’s douing. For hie’s bign gaon tou lòng,
uel past þe tîme hie seûd've tâcan.
But uene hie comes, I’ll bie a uicad man
if I dou not acte on all þe god revieles.
