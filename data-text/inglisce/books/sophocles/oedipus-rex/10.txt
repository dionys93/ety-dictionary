PRIEST

Lete os gait [up], c̃ildren. For þis man
has [willingly] declâred jost uate uie câme for.
And máit Phoebus, hu sent þis [oracle],
come as aur [saviour] and [end] aur [sickness].

CHORUS

[O] suiete spieching voice [of] Zeus,
you have com to [glorious] Thebes from [golden] Pytho—
but uate is your intente?
My fiarful harte [twists] on þe rac and seacs uiþ fiare.
[O] Delian [healer], for [whom] uie crîe [aloud]
in holy [awe], uate [obligation]
uill you demande from mi, [a] þingue ungnone
or nôe renoued uiþ the revolving years?
[Immortal] voice, [O] chîld [of] [golden] Hope,
spieche to mi!

[First] I calle on you, Athena þe [immortal],
dautre [of] Zeus, and on your sistre, tou,
Artemis, hu gardes aur [land] and sits
on her [glorious] rônd þrone in aur [market] plâce,
and on Phoebus, hu seoutes from [far] [away].
[O] you three gardians against daiþ,
appire to mi!
[If] before nôe you have evre drivan off
[a] fîery plâgue to chiepe desastre
from þe citie and have banișed it,
[then] come to os þis tîme as uel!

[Alas], þe pains I beire are [numberless]—
my pípuls nôe all [sick] uiþ plâgue,
aur mŷnds cane fînde no uaipons
to helpe uiþ aur [defence]. Nôe þe [offspring]
[of] aur [splendid] Erþ no lòng groe,
[nor] dou aur uimmen cryhing aut in [labour]
gait þeir reliefe from [a] lîving noueórcor-born chîld.
As you cane sihe—uan by uan þey [swoop] [away],
off to þe seors [of] þe eivening [god], lîc birds
fast þan fŷre uic̃ no uan cane resiste.
