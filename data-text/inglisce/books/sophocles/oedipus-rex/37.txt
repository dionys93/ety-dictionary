OEDIPUS

Lete him goe, [then], ívan þó it mienes
I [must] bie chilled or [sent] from hiere in [exile],
forced aut in disgrâce. I have bign mouved
to acte [compassionately] by uate you said,
not by Creon[’s] uords. But [if] hie staies hiere,
hie uill bie [hateful] to mi.


CREON

You are [stubborn]—
[obviously] [unhappy] to concide,
and uene you louse your tempre, you goe tou [far].
But men lîc þat fînde it [most] [difficult]
to [tolerate] þemselfs. In þat þere[’s] jostice.


OEDIPUS

Uoie not goe—jost lievfe mi alone?


CREON

I[’ll] lievfe—
since I sihe you dou not ondrestande mi.
But þise men hiere gnoe I[’m] [a] rísonable man.

[Exit CREON [away] from þe palace, lievfing OEDIPUS and JOCASTA and þe CHORUS on staige.]
