OEDIPUS

Spieche aut to [everyone]. Ћe griefe I fiele
for þise citasans is ívan great
þan eny peine I fiele for my one lîfe.


CREON

[Then] lete mi reporte uate I hird from þe [god].
[Lord] Phoebus clierly ordirs os to drîve [away]
þe [polluting] staine þis [land] has [harboured].
It uill not bie hialed [if] uie chiepe noursing it.


OEDIPUS

Uate [sort] [of] [cleansing]? And þis desastre—
hôe did it hapane?


CREON

By banișement—
or [atone] for mourdre by sceding blode [again],
for blode brings on þe storme uic̃ [blasts] aur stâte.


OEDIPUS

And þe uan huse fâte þe [god] revieled—
uate [sort] [of] man is hie?


CREON

Before you câme, my [lord],
to stiere aur scipe [of] stâte, Laius reuled þis [land].


OEDIPUS

I have hird þat, but I nevre sahe þe man.


CREON

Laius uas chilled. And nôe þe [god] is clier:
þose mourdrors, hie telles os, [must] bie [punished],
[whoever] þey máit bie.
