CRÉON

Gûd nous, I telle you. If þings uirc aut uel,
þen þise trobils, so difficult to beire,
uill ende up bringuing os great benefits.


EDIPUS

Uate is þi oracle? So far your uords
inspîre in mi no confidence or fiar.


CRÉON

If you uisce to hyre þe nous in public,
I’m prepared to spieche. Or uie coûd steppe insîde.
 

EDIPUS

Spiec aut to evryoan. Ћe griefe I fiele
for þise citasans is iven greatar
þan eny peine I fiele for myone lîfe.


CRÉON

Ћen let-mi reporte uate I hêd from þe god.
Lorde Phiebus clierly ordres os to drîve aüay
þe polluting staigne þis lande has harbored.
It uill not bie hialed if uie chiepe noursing it.


EDIPUS

Uate sorte of clainsing? And þis desastre—
hôe did it hapane?


CRÉON

By banișement—
or atone for mourdre by sceding blode again,
for blood brings on the storme uic̃ blastes aur stâte.


EDIPUS

And þe uan huse fâte þe god revieled—
uate sorte of man is hie?


CRÉON

Before you câme, my lorde,
to stiere aur scipe of stâte, Láios reuled this lande.


EDIPUS

I've hêd þat, but I nevre sah þe man.
