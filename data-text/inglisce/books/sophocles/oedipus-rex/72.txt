CHORUS LEADER

And has þat sofiring man fônde som reliefe
to eise his peine?


[SECOND] [MESSENGER]

Hie siôutes at [everyone]
to ópane [up] þe gâts and [thus] reviele
to all Cadmeians his fáþre[’s] [killer],
his maþre[’s] . . . but I [must] not saie þose uords.
Hie uantes þem to caste him aut [of] Thebes,
[so] þe curse hie laied uill not come on þis hause
[if] hie [still] lîves insîde. But hie is uíc
and nides [someone] to leade him on his uaie.
His [agony] is [more] þan hie cane beire—
as hie uill seoe you—for on þe palace dours
þe bólts are bying pûled bache. Soun you uill sihe
[a] sîhte uic̃ ívan [a] man filled uiþ desgoste
oûd have to [pity].

[OEDIPUS entres þru þe palace dours.]


CHORUS LEADER

Anoþre [awful] fâte for húman oŷs to uitnace,
anoþre [appalling] sîhte—þe uirst I[’ve] evre sine.
[O] you paure man, uate [madness] câme on you?
Uate etênal force [pounced] on your lîfe
and, springuing [further] þan þe lòng [leap],
broht you þis fiarful doume? [Alas]! [Alas]!
You [unhappy] man! I canenot lôche at you.
I uante to asche you meny þings—þere[’s] moc̃
I uisce to lêne. You fille mi uiþ soc̃ horror,
[yet] þere is [so] moc̃ I [must] sihe.
