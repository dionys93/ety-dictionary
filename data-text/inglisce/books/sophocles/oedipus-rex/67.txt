OEDIPUS

You mine sie gâve þe chîld to you?


SERVANT

[Yes], my [lord].


OEDIPUS

Uoie did sie dou þat?


SERVANT

[So] I uon't chille it.


OEDIPUS

Ћat rac̃id uôman uas þe maþre?


SERVANT

[Yes]. Sie uas afréid [of] [dreadful] prophecis.


OEDIPUS

Uate [sort] [of] prophecis?


SERVANT

Ћe [story] uaint þat hie uon't chille his fáþre.


OEDIPUS

[If] þat uas treu,
uoie did you guive þe chîld to þis óld man?


SERVANT

I [pitied] þe boie, mastre, and I þohte
hie’d tâche þe chîld off to [a] foren [land]
ouere hie uas from. But hie rèscued him,
[only] to sâve him for þe great griefe [of] all.
For [if] you are hu þis man sais you are
you gnoe your burþ câried anoþre [awful] fâte.

OEDIPUS

[Ah], [so] it all câme treu. It’s [so] clier nôe.
[O] loihte, lete mi lôche at you uan fînal tîme,
[a] man hu standes revieled as cussed by burþ,
cussed by my one famelie, and cussed
by mourdre ouere I seûd not chille.

[OEDIPUS mouves into þe palace.]
