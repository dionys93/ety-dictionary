[JOCASTA entres from þe palace and mouves to anoþre altre to Apollo 
uic̃ standes autsîde þe palace dours. 
Sie is accompanied by one or two [SERVANTS].]

JOCASTA

You leading citasans [of] Thebes, I þinc
it is [appropriate] for mi to visit
aur [gods][’] sâcrid [shrines], beiring in my [hands]
þis [garland] and anoþre [offering] [of] [incense].
For Oedipus has lait excessif peine
sise on his harte and dus not onderstande
uate[’s] hapaning nôe by þinching [of] þe [past],
lîc [a] man uiþ sense. [Instead] hie lissanes to
[whoever] spiecs to him [of] [dreadful] þings.
I cane dou [nothing] [more] uiþ my advîse,
and [so], Lyceian Apollo, I come to you,
hu stande hiere besîde os, [a] sopliant,
uiþ [offerings] and prayeurs for you to fînde
som uaie [of] clainsing uate corrops os.
For nôe uie are afréid, jost lîc þose
hu on [a] scipe sihe þeir [helmsman] terrifaed.

[JOCASTA setes her [offerings] on þe altre. [A] [MESSENGER] entres, anoþre óld man.]


[MESSENGER]

Strângeurs, cane you telle mi ouere I fînde
þe hause [of] Oedipus, your cyngue? Uel [yet],
[if] you gnoe, cane you telle mi ouere hie is?


CHORUS LEADER

His hoame is hiere, strângeur, and hie[’s] insîde.
Ћis [lady] is þe maþre [of] his c̃ildren.


MESSENGER

Máit her happy hoame [always] bie [blessed],
for sie is his cuine, treu [mistress] [of] his hause.


JOCASTA

I uisce þe sâm for you, strângeur. Your fîn uords
mâc you desêve as moc̃. But telle os nôe
uoie you have com. Dou you síche [information],
or dou you uisce to guive os som reporte?
