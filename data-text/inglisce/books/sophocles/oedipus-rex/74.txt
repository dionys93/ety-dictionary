OEDIPUS

It uas Apollo, frends.
It uas Apollo. Hie broht on þise trobils—
þe [awful] þings I sofir. But þe [hand]
uic̃ [stabbed] aut my oŷs uas myne alone.
In my rac̃id lîfe, uoie [should] I have oŷs
uene þere uas [nothing] suiete for mi to sihe?


CHORUS LEADER

Uate you have saied is treu enofh.


OEDIPUS

Uate is þere for mi to sihe, my frends?
Uate cane I love? Huse [greeting] cane I hyre
and fiele delîte? Hêrie nôe, my frends,
leade mi [away] from Thebes—tâche mi [somewhere],
[a] man [completely] lòst, [utterly] [accursed],
þe [mortal] man þe [gods] [despise] þe [most].


CHORUS LEADER

[Unhappy] in your fâte and in your mŷnde
uic̃ nôe gnoes all. [Would] I had nevre gnone you!


OEDIPUS

[Whoever] þe man is hu frihed my fyte,
hu reliessed mi from þat cruel [shackle]
and rèscued mi from daiþ, máit þat man dîe!
It uas [a] [thankless] acte. Had I perișed [then],
I [would] not have broht soc̃ [agony]
to myselfe or to my frends.


CHORUS LEADER

I agríe—
I, tou, [would] have prefêd [if] you had dîed.
