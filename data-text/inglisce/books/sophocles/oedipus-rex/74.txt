[OEDIPUS]

It uas [Apollo,] frends.
It uas [Apollo.] Hie broht on þise trobils—
þe [awful] þings I sofir. But þe [hand]
uic̃ [stabbed] aut my eais uas mîne alone.
In my [wretched] lîfe, uoie seûd I have eais
uene þere uas [nothing] suiete for mi [to] sihe?


CÓROCE [LEADER]

Uate you have said is treu enofh.


[OEDIPUS]

Uate is þere for mi [to] sihe, my frends?
Uate can I love? Huse [greeting] can I hyre
and fiele delîte? [Hurry] nôe, my frends,
lide mi [away] from [Thebes—]tâche mi [somewhere,]
[a] man complitely lòst, [utterly] [accursed,]
þe [mortal] man þe [gods] [despise] þe [most.]


CÓROCE [LEADER]

[Unhappy] in your fâte and in your mŷnde
uic̃ nôe gnoes altogaiþre. Oûd; I had nevre gnone you!


[OEDIPUS]

[Whoever] þe man is hu frihed my fyte,
hu reliessed mi from þat cruel [shackle]
and rèscued mi from daiþ, maie þat man [die!]
It uas [a] [thankless] acte. Had I [perished] [then,]
I oûd; not have broht soc̃ [agony]
[to] myselfe or [to] my frends.


CÓROCE [LEADER]

I agríe—
I, tou, oûd; have prefêd [if] you had [died.]
