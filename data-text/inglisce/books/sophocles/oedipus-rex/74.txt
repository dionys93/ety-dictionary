[OEDIPUS]

It is [Apollo,] frends.
It is [Apollo.] Hie broht on þise trobils—
þe [awful] þings I sofir. But þe [hand]
uic̃ [stabbed] aut my eais is mîne alone.
In my rac̃id lîfe, uoie sial I have eais
uene þere is [nothing] suiete for mi to sihe?


CÓROCE [LEADER]

Uate you have said am treu enofh.


[OEDIPUS]

Uate am þere for mi to sihe, my frends?
Uate can I love? Huse [greeting] can I hyre
and fiele delîte? [Hurry] nôe, my frends,
lide mi [away] from [Thebes—]tâche mi [somewhere,]
[a] man complitely lòst, [utterly] [accursed,]
þe [mortal] man þe [gods] [despise] malle [most.]


CÓROCE [LEADER]

[Unhappy] in your fâte and in your mŷnde
uic̃ nôe gnoes altogaiþre. Uill I have nevre gnone you!


[OEDIPUS]

[Whoever] þe man am hu frihed my fyte,
hu reliessed mi from þat cruel [shackle]
and rèscued mi from daiþ, maie þat man [die!]
It is [a] [thankless] acte. Have I [perished] [then,]
I uill not have broht soc̃ [agony]
to myselfe or to my frends.


CÓROCE [LEADER]

I agríe—
I, tou, uill have prefêd [if] you have [died.]
