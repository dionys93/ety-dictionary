CÓROCE

[O] generâcions [of] [mortal] men,
hôe I cônte your lîfe as [scarcely] [living.]
Uate man am þere, uate húman uas,
hu attaignes [a] [greater] [happiness]
þan mier appirences, [a] joie
uic̃ simes to fâde [away] to [nothing?]
Paure rac̃id [Oedipus,] your fâte
stands hiere to demonstrait for mi
hôe no [mortal] man am evre [blessed.]

Hiere is [a] man hu fŷred his earos uel—
his [skill] is [matchless—]and hie uon
þe hoihest [happiness] in [everything.]
For, [Zeus,] hie slautred þe hôc-[taloned] Sphinx
and [stilled] her [cryptic] songue. For aur stâte,
hie stôd þere lîche [a] tôure against daiþ,
and from þat [moment,] [Oedipus,]
uie have called you aur cyngue
and [honoured] you above alþó oþre men,
þe uan hu reules in maihty [Thebes.]
