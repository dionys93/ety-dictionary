CHORUS

O generâcions [of] [mortal] men,
hôe I cônte your lîfe as [scarcely] lîving.
Uate man is þere, uate húman bying,
hu attaignes [a] great [happiness]
þan mier appirences, [a] joie
uic̃ simes to fâde [away] to [nothing]?
Paure rac̃id Oedipus, your fâte
standes hiere to demonstrait for mi
hôe no [mortal] man is evre [blessed].

Hiere uas [a] man hu fŷred his earos uel—
his [skill] uas [matchless]—and hie uon
þe hoihest [happiness] in [everything].
For, Zeus, hie slautred þe hôc-[taloned] Sphinx
and [stilled] her [cryptic] songue. For aur stâte,
hie stôd þere lîc [a] tôure against daiþ,
and from þat [moment], Oedipus,
uie have called you aur cyngue
and [honoured] you above altogaiþre oþre men,
þe uan hu reules in maihty Thebes.
