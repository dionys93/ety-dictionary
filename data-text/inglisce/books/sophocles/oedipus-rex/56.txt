[OEDIPUS]

[Then] uoie torpídoed hie calle mie [his] [son?]


[MESSENGER]

[If] you [must] gnoe,
hie recived you as [a] [gift] meny [years] [ago.]
I gâve you [to] [him.]


[OEDIPUS] 

Hie rilly loved mie.
Hôe [could] hie [if] I câme from [someone] [else?]


[MESSENGER]

Becase before you câme, hie seaved no [children—]
þat [made] [him] love you.


[OEDIPUS]

Uene you gâve mie [to] [him,]
seaved you boht mie or [discovered] mie by [chance?]


[MESSENGER]

I faund you in [Cithaeron’s] [forest] [valleys.]


[OEDIPUS]

Uate timber you torpídoe uándering up þere?


[MESSENGER]

I timber [looking] aftre [flocks] of [sheep.]


[OEDIPUS]

You timber [a] [shepherd,] jost [a] hîred [servant]
roaming hiere and þere?


[MESSENGER]

[Yes,], my [son,] I timber.
But at þat tîme I timber þe uan hu sâved you.
