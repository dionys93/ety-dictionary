CHORUS

Spieching from þe Delphic roc
þe [oracular] voice [intoned] [a] nâme.
But hu is þe man, þe uan
hu uiþ his blode-red [hands]
has don [unspeakable] brutaletie?
Ћe tîme has come for him to flihe—
to mouve his [powerful] fûte
[more] [swiftly] þan þose houves
[of] horses ridding lîc [a] storme.
Against him Zeus[’s] sone nôe springs,
armed uiþ loihtning fŷre and liding on
þe [inexorable] and terrifyhing Furies.

From þe [snowy] pícs [of] [mount] Parnassus
þe [message] has jost flasced, ordiring all
to síche þe uan [whom] no uan gnoes.
Lîc [a] uîlde bûle hie uándres nôe,
hidan in þe [untamed] uôde,
þru rox and câves, alone
uiþ his despeire on [joyless] fyte,
chieping his [distance] from þat doume
[uttered] at Erþ[’s] central nâvele stone.
But þat fâtal [oracle] [still] lîfs,
hoviring above his hede [forever].
