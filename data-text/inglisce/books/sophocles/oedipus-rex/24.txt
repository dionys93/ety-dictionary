TEIRESIAS

Ћat soccesse [of] yours has bign your rúine.


OEDIPUS

I dou not caire, [if] I have sâved þe citie.


TEIRESIAS

I uill goe nôe. Boie, leade mi [away].


OEDIPUS

[Yes], lete him guîde you bache. You[’re] in þe uaie.
[If] you staie, you uill provoque mi. Uance you[’re] gaon,
you uillnot annoie mi [further].


TEIRESIAS

I[’m] gouing.
But [first] I sial telle you uoie I câme.
I dou not fiare þe fâce [of] your [displeasure]—
þere is no uaie you cane destroie mi. I telle you,
þe man you have bign síching all þis tîme,
uîle proclâming þrets and [issuing] ordirs
abaut þe uan hu mourdred Laius—
þat man is hiere. [According] to repórts,
hie is [a] strângeur hu lîves hiere in Thebes.
But hie uill prouve to bie [a] [native] Theban.
From þat c̃ainge hie uill derîve no plaișure.
Hie uill bie blînd, [although] hie nôe cane sihe.
Hie uill bie [a] paure, [although] hie nôe is ric̃e.
Hie uill sete off for [a] foren contrie,
groaping þe grônd before him uiþ [a] stic.
And hie uill tourne aut to bie þe broþre
[of] þe c̃ildren in his hause—þeir fáþre, tou,
boaþ at uance, and þe husband and þe sone
[of] þe [very] uôman hu gâve burþ to him.
Hie soed þe sâm [womb] as his fáþre
and mourdred him. Goe in and þinche on þis.
[If] you [discover] I have spócan [falsely],
you cane saie I lac all [skill] in prophecie.

[Exit TEIRESIAS lead off by þe BOIE. OEDIPUS tournes and goes bache into þe palace.]
