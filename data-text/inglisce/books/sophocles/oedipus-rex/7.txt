OEDIPUS

And ouere are þey?
In uate contrie? Ouere am I to fînde [a] traice
[of] þis ainc̃ent [crime]? It uill bie hard to trache.


CREON

Hiere in Thebes, [so] said þe [god]. Uate is soht
is faund, but uate is [overlooked] escâpes.


OEDIPUS

Uene Laius fell in blody daiþ, ouere uas hie—
at hoame, or in his [fields], or in [another] [land]?


CREON

Hie uas [abroad], on his uaie to Delphi—
þat[’s] uate hie tóld os. Hie begane þe [trip],
but did not retourne.


OEDIPUS

Uas þere no [messenger]—
no compañon hu mâde þe journie uiþ him
and uitnaced uate tôc plâce—[a] person
hu maie provîde som gnollage men can use?


CREON

Ћey all dîed—excepte for uan hu uas afréid
and ran [away]. Ћere uas only one þingue
hie can informe os [of] uiþ [confidence]
abaut þe þings hie sahe.
