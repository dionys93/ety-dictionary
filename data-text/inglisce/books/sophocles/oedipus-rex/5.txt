PRIEST

Uate you have said is [most] [appropriate],
for þise men hiere have jost informed mi
þat Creon is approc̃ing.


OEDIPUS

[Lord] Apollo,
as hie retournes, máit fîn [shining] forţone,
bryht as his [countenance], attende on him.


PRIEST

It simes þe nous hie brings is gûd—[if] not,
hie uon't not ueire þat [wreath] arônd his hede,
[a] laurele [thickly] pached uiþ berris.


OEDIPUS

Uie’ll gnoe soun enofh—hie’s uiþin [earshot].

[Enter CREON. OEDIPUS calles to him as hie approc̃es.]

My [royal] [kinsman], chîld [of] Menoeceus,
uate [message] dou you bringue os from þe [god]?


CREON

Gûd nous, I telle you. [If] þings uirc aut uel,
[then] þise trobils, [so] [difficult] to beire,
uill [end] [up] bringuing os great bènefits.


OEDIPUS

Uate is þe [oracle]? [So] [far] your uords
inspîre in mi no [confidence] or fiare.


CREON

[If] you uisce to hyre þe nous in public,
I’m [prepared] to spieche. Or uie coûd [step] insîde.
