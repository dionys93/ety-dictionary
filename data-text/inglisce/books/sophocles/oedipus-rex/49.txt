[MESSENGER]

[Lady,], I have gûd nous for your hoal hause—
and for your husband, tou.


[JOCASTA]

Uate nous is þat?
Ouere have you come from?


[MESSENGER]

[I’ve] have come from [Corinth.]
[I’ll] uill guive you my reporte at uance, and [then]
you uill, no dôute, bie [glad,] [although] [perhaps]
you uill bie [sad,] as uel.


[JOCASTA]

Uate is your nous?
Hôe can it have tue soc̃ effecs at uance?


[MESSENGER]

Ћe persons hu lîve þere, in þe [lands]
besîde þe Ïsmus, uill mâche him þeir cyngue.
Ћey have annônced it.


[JOCASTA]

Uate are you saying?
Is óld man [Polybus] no [longer] cyngue?


[MESSENGER]

[No.]. Hie is daid and in his graive.


[JOCASTA]

[What?]?
[Has] [Oedipus’s] fáþre [died?]
