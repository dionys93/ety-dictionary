OEDIPUS

No, no. It[’s] public gnollage. Loxias
once said it uas my fâte þat I uill mârie
my one maþre and scede my fáþre[’s] blode
uiþ my one [hands]. Ћat[’s] uoie, many years ago,
I laift my hoame in Corinth. Ћings tourned aut uel,
but [nonetheless] it guives þe suietest joie
to lôche into þe eais [of] uan[’s] one pârents.


MESSENGER

And becase you uere afréid [of] her
you staied [away] from Corinth?


OEDIPUS

And becase I did not uante to bie my fáþre[’s] [killer].


MESSENGER

My [lord], since I câme to mâche you happy,
uoie dou I not relieve you [of] þis fiare?


OEDIPUS

You uill recive from mi [a] [worthy] þaincs.


MESSENGER

Ћat[’s] rilly uoie I câme—[so] your retourne
maie prouve [a] bènefit to mi bache hoame.


OEDIPUS

But I uill nevre goe bache to my pârents.


MESSENGER

My sone, it is [so] clier you[’ve] no îdía
uate you are douing . . .


OEDIPUS [interrupting]

Uate dou you miene, óld man?
In þe nâme [of] altogaiþre þe [gods], telle mi.
