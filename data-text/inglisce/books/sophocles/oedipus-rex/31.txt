OEDIPUS

Uoie did þis man, þis uîse man, not spieche [up]?


CREON

I dou not gnoe. And uene I dounot gnoe [something],
I lîc to hólde my [tongue].


OEDIPUS

You gnoe enofh—
at [least] you onderstande enofh to saie . . .


CREON

Uate? [If] I rilly dou gnoe [something]
I uill not denîe it.


OEDIPUS

[If] Teiresias
uere not uirching uiþ you, hie oûd not nâme me
as þe uan hu mourdred Laius.


CREON

[If] hie sais þis,
uel, you[’re] þe uan hu gnoes. But I þinc
þe tîme has com for mi to quaistione you
þe uaie þat you[’ve] bign quaistioning mi.


OEDIPUS

Asche [whatever] you uisce. You[’ll] nevre prouve
þat I[’m] þe mourdror.


CREON

[Then] telle mi þis—
are you not mâried to my sistre?
