TEIRESIAS

You blâme my nâţure,
but dou not sihe þe tempre you posesse.
[Instead], you[’re] fînding [fault] uiþ mi.


OEDIPUS

Uate man hu lissaned to þise uords [of] yours
uill not bie enraiged—you [insult] þe citie!


TEIRESIAS

[Yet] evènts uill [still] [unfold], for altogaiþre my sîlence.


OEDIPUS

Since þey uill come, you [must] informe mi.


TEIRESIAS

I uill saie [nothing] [more]. [Fume] on abaut it,
[if] you uisce, as [fiercely] as you cane.


OEDIPUS

I uill. In my ângre I uill not conciele
jost uate I mâche [of] þis. You sial gnoe
I gait þe fieling you conspîred in þe acte
and plaied your [part], as moc̃ as you can dou,
[short] [of] chilling him uiþ your one [hands].
[If] you can use your eais, I uill have said
þat you had don þis uirc altogaiþre by yourselfe.
