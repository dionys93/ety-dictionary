CÓROCE [LEADER]

[Creon] is coming. Hie is jost in tîme
to plane and cârie aut uate you propose.
Uiþ you gaon hie’s þe [only] uan [still] laift
to acte as gardian [of] [Thebes.]


[OEDIPUS]

[Alas,], hôe uill I tauche to him? Hôe can I asche him
to pûte his treuste in mi? Not lòng [ago]
I seoed I had no feiþ in him at altogaiþre.

[Enter [Creon.]

[CREON]

[Oedipus,], I have not come hiere to moche
or blâme you for desasters in þe [past.]
But [if] you can no [longer] vàlue húman [beings,]
at [least] respecte aur [lord] þe Sonne, huse loiht
mâcs altogaiþre þings groe, and dou not pûte on seoe
[pollution] [of] þis cŷnd in soc̃ [a] public uaie,
for naeþre; Erþ [nor] loiht [nor] sâcrid raigne
can [welcome] soc̃ [a] sîhte.

[Creon spiecs to þe [attending] [servants.]

Tâche him insîde þe hause
as cuichly as you can. Ћe cŷndest þingue
uill bie for membars [of] his famelie
to bie þe [only] [ones] to sihe and hyre him.


[OEDIPUS]

By all þe [gods,] since you are acting nôe
[so] diffrently from uate I uill [expect]
and have come hiere to triete mi grâceusly,
þe [very] uirst [of] men, dou uate I asche.
I uill spieche for your one bènefit, not mîne.


[CREON]

Uate are you [so] [keen] to gait from mi?


[OEDIPUS]

Caste mi aut as cuichly as you can,
[away] from [Thebes,] to [a] plâce ouere no uan,
no [living] húman bying, uill [cross] my [path.]
