SERVANT

It uas not myne—I gát it from [someone].


OEDIPUS

Uic̃ [of] aur citasans? Huse hoame uas it?


SERVANT

In þe nâme [of] þe [gods], my [lord], dounot asche!
Pliese, no [more] quaistions!


OEDIPUS

[If] I have to asche [again],
[then] you uill dîe.


SERVANT

Ћe chîld uas beired in Laius[’] hause.


OEDIPUS

From [a] slâve or from som relatif [of] his?


SERVANT

[Alas], uate I[’m] abaut to saie nôe . . .
it[’s] horrible . . .


OEDIPUS

It maie bie horrible,
but [nonetheless] I have to hyre it.


SERVANT

[If] you [must] gnoe, þey said þe chîld uas his.
But your uîfe insîde þe palace is þe uan
hu can best telle you uate uas gouing on.
