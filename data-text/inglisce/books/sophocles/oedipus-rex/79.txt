OEDIPUS

I uisce you uel.
And for þis acte, maie þe [god] uac̃he ovre you
and triete you uel þan hie trieted mi.
[Ah], my c̃ildren, ouere are you? Come hiere,
come into my arms—you are my sistors nôe—
fiele þise [hands] uic̃ tourned your fáþre[’s] eais,

þise [empty] [sockets]. Hie uas [a] man hu,
sahing [nothing], gnouing [nothing], fáþored you
uiþ þe uôman hu had guivan burþ to him.
I uiepe for you. [Although] I canenot sihe,
I þinche abaut your lîfe in days to come,
þe bitre lîfe uic̃ men uill force on you.
Uate citasans uill assoçiait uiþ you?
Uate fiestes uill you [attend] and not come hoame
in teirs, uiþ no seare in þe [rejoicing]?
Uene you[’re] maţure enofh for mârage,
hu uill bie þere for you, my c̃ildren,
uate husband raidy to assoume þe seame

Uate pervêsion is not [manifest] in os?

Ћose are þe [insults] þey uill hurle at you.
Hu, [then], uill mârie you? No uan, my c̃ildren.
You [must] [wither], bairan and [unmarried].

Dou not lete þem lîve as [vagrant] [paupers],
uándering arônd [unmarried]. You are

deprîved [of] [everything] excepte [a] seare
