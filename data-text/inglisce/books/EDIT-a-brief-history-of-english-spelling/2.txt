Ћi Inglisce r̃aiting sistem

Inglisce has grone from þe lânguage broht to Britan in þe 5þ cențurie by Ânglo-Saxon invâdeurs from Norþ Gêmanie. 
Its historie is úșally divîded into þri maign faises:

Óld Inglisce – from þi arrîval of þi invâdeurs in þe 5þ cențurie to arônd 1130
Middle Inglisce – rofhly 1130 to 1470
Modern Inglisce – abaut 1470 to þe present

Hôuevre þere uere meny c̃ainges uiþin eic̃ fâse – for example Êly Modern Inglisce (rofhly 1470 to 1700) is sine as distínct from treuly Modern Inglisce. 
In rialetie, of corse, c̃ainge has bign ongouing þru all þe fâses.

Ћe Róman alphabet and Latin uere used in Britan uene it uas parte of þe Róman Empîar (AD 43 to 410), 
and þey staied in use in þe Cailtic parts of þe Britisce Îals aftre moast of þe Rómans laift.

Hôuevre, þi invâdeurs broht uiþ'em þe rounic alphabet, gnone as þe fouþorc from its first six lettars. 
A feu small exampels of Óld Inglisce r̃itan in rouns have sorvîved. 
Ћere uere at þat tîme alrèdy sevral distínct Inglisce dîalecs bâssed rofhly on þe seprat cyngdoms of Ânglo-Saxon Ingland.

In 597 St Augustine câme from Rome to Canterburie and converted þe Saxons in Quente to Cristianetie. 
After þis þe Crisțan moncs started using þe Róman alphabet to r̃aite Inglisce. 
As þe Róman alphabet did not have enofh lettars, þey also used som rouns, soc̃ as Þ (called þorne) for þe "þ" sônds in þis and þin. 
At þis staige, Inglisce spelling uas moastly fairly simple, as þe lettars mac̃ed þe spocan uords quît uel.

Mâjor sorvîving uêx in Óld Inglisce include þi "Anglo-Saxon Chronicle", Bede's "History of the Christian Church in England" (translaited into Óld Inglisce from Bede's Latin), and þe sága Beowulf.
