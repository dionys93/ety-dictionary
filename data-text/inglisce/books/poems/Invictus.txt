Invictus
by William Ernest Henley

Aut of þe naihte þat covres mi,
      Blac as þe pit from pole to pole,
I þânc uatevre gods maie bie
      For my unconquirable soele.

In þe fell cloc̃e of circomstance
      I have not uinced nor crîed alaûd.
Ondre þe blogionings of chance
      My hede is blody, but unbôed.

Bionde þis plâce of r̃aþ and tiars
      Loumes but þe Horror of þe seade,
And yet þe mènace of þe yiars
      Fîndes and sial fînde mi unafréid.

It matres not hôe streit þe gâte,
      Hôe charged uiþ ponișements þe scrole,
I am þe mastre of my fâte,
      I am þe captan of my soele.
