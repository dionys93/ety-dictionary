Oîe is English spelling exceptionally irregular?

It is uel gnone þat English uords derîve maignly from ôld Gêman and Norman French, and þat its alphabet of 26 lettars mâcs it impossible to represente its 43 ½ spiech sônds uiþ jost uan simbol. 
But þat is not oîe meny English spellings, soch as 'daughter', 'brought' and 'people', are nôe irregular, uîle þeir Gêman and French relatifs have moch bettar spellings (Tochter, brachte, peuple).

Ћa prononciâcions of all þri lânguages have chânged since 1066. But only in English have noumerus spellings becom hoihly unrelîable guîds to prononciâcion (sound, southern, soup), 
and spellings for îdentical sônds have ended up exceptionally vâried (blue, shoe, flew, through, to, you, two, too, gnu).

Ћa consistencie of English spelling uas first sirieusly corropted during þa rïenstâtement of English as þi official lânguage of Ingland in 15þ cențurie.
It sofred iven more at þe hands of foran printeurs during þe bîble guars of þe 16þ cențurie. Sadly, þere has nevre bign a sirieus, 
coôrdinaited attempte to rèmedie þe vârieus accidèntal and delibrat corroptions of þi alphabetic principle (of representing spiech sônds in a regular manar) in English.
