The Cedilla in Inglisce

ç /s/ before a, o, u; /ʃ/ before e and i
d̦ /ʤ/
ș /ʃ/
ț /ʧ/ between two vowels or xț + vowel; /ʃ/ when preceded by a consonant

In Inglisce, the cedilla (ç) is used on the letter c in specific situations to indicate a change in pronunciation.

    ç is pronounced /s/ before a, o, and consonants.
    ç is pronounced /ʃ/ before i, e, and u.

1. Verb Endings and Agent Nouns:

    When a verb ends in -ce and the agent noun is inanimate:
        to juce → þe juçor
        to spâce → þe spâçor

    When -able or -ability are added to the stem:
        to slîce → slîçable

2. Verbs Ending in -içe:

    English: to finish → Inglisce: to finișe
    English: to publish → Inglisce: to publiçe
    English: to abolish → Inglisce: to abólișe

3. Adjectives of French/Latin Origin:

    Some adjectives of French or Latin origin also use ç:
        to sofîce → sofiçent
        profiçent, effiçent, omniçent, pâçent

        act → acțual
        effect → effecțual
        fact → facțual
        intellect → intellecțual
