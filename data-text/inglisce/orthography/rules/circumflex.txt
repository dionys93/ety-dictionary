The Circumflex Accent in Inglisce

In Inglisce, the circumflex accent is used on the following vowels: â, î, ê, ô, û. 
Circumflexes represent vowel sound first, and stress second. The gender will tell you whether the circumflex represents just the sound or the stress.

The primary IPA pronunciations for each vowel with a circumflex are:

    Ââ: /eɪ/
    Îî: /aɪ/
    Êê: /ər/
    Ôô: /oʊ/, /æʊ/, /ʊ/
    Ûû: /ʊ/

Examples:

    burn → bêne
    confirm → confirme
    cushion → cûcion
    decide → decîde
    earn → êne
    hypnosis → hipnôsis
    location → locâcion


The vowels î, ê, ô, û can have secondary pronunciations when combined with certain vowels or consonants:

    aî: /ɛ/
    ô: /æʊ/ before -n and vowels, /ʊ/ before -c
    oû: /wʊ/ without a consonant before it, /ʊ/ after consonants
    aû: /æʊ/

Examples:

    compare, comparison, comparable → compaire, compaîrasson, compaîrable
    impair, impairment → empeire, empeirement
    iron → îarn
    wear, wearable → ueire, ueirable
    would, could, should → oûd, coûd, seûd
    how → hôe
    cow, vow → côe, vôe
