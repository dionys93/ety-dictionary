The "-ar" Ending in Inglish

The -ar suffix is used in both adjectives and nouns, though its application varies depending on the word's etymology. 
In adjectives, it is often found in words that can take the -ety suffix, suggesting a Latin origin. 
In nouns, the -ar ending typically originates from Germanic or Middle Eastern languages.

1. Adjectives with "-ar":

Adjectives that end in -ar often derive from Latin and can form nouns by adding the -ity suffix.

Examples:

    regula, regularis [Latin]
    regular, regularity [Modern English]
    reg̃ular, reg̃ulârety [Inglish]
    
    particula, particularis [Latin]
    particular, particularity [Modern English]
    partic̃ular, partic̃ulârety [Inglish]

    familia, familiaris [Latin]
    familiar, familiarity [Modern English]
    familhar, familhârety [Inglish]

These adjectives maintain the -ar ending in Inglish, preserving their Latin roots while allowing for consistency in their noun forms.

2. Nouns with "-ar":

Nouns ending in -ar are typically of Germanic or Middle Eastern origin. In Inglish, these nouns retain their -ar ending, reflecting their etymological roots.

Examples:

    summer → somar
    winter → wintar
    sugar → s̃ugar
    weather → weþar

This change creates a distinct spelling pattern for these nouns, while maintaining their original sound and form.
