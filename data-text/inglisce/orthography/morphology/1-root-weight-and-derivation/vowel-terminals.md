-ie verbs follow the pattern -ies -ied and -ying.

These verbs include the following:
to mârie (to marry)
to bârie (to bury)
to uírie (to weary)
to cârie (to carry)
to copie (to copy)
to studie (to study)
to hêrie (to hurry)
to uêrie (to worry)

Related forms:
mârage (marriage)
bârial (burial)
beiro (barrow)
cârage (carrage)
copie, copis (copy, copies)
studie, studis (study, studies)
student -s
hêrie (hurry)
uêrie, uêris

---

-oie verbs drop the -ie and add -ying in their progressive tense.
These verbs include the following:
to annoie (to annoy)
to emploie (to employ)
to enjoie (to enjoy)
to convoie (to convoy)
to deploie (to deploy)
to destroie (to destroy)
