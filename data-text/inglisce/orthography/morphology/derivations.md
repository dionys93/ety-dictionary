## The Lean Verb / Heavy Noun Split (-it, -id)

In Modern English, words ending in a short `/ɪ/` sound (spelled **-it**, **-id**, or **-yt**) frequently suffer from zero-derivation, where the exact same spelling is used for both an active verb (to *pit*) and a concrete noun (the *pit*). 

**Inglisce** eliminates this visual ambiguity by treating the spelling as a grammatical flag. Regardless of whether the word comes from Old English or Latin, the system relies on a universal rule: **Lean verbs indicate actions, while heavy spellings indicate concrete nouns or stressed structural roots.**

---

### 1. Monosyllables (The Germanic Base)

Native Germanic verbs maintain a lean root (no terminal decorations) to indicate the active verb. When these lean verbs zero-derive into a physical, concrete object, Inglisce applies morphological weight. The terminal consonant is doubled and capped with an `-e` (e.g., `-itte`, `-ytte`).

To handle homophones (words that sound identical but mean different things), Inglisce utilizes the **y** substitution in specific roots to visually isolate them from one another.

#### Regular Conjugations
These verbs take standard suffixation (e.g., *-ed*) in their past tense.

| Inglisce Verb | Conjugations | Derived Noun | Definitions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to **pit** | pit, pits, pitted, pitting | **pitte**, **pits** *(pl.)* | *Verb:* To extract a seed.<br>*Noun:* The hard seed of a fruit. |
| to **spit** | spit, spits, spitted, spitting | **spitte**, **spits** *(pl.)* | *Verb:* To pierce meat for roasting.<br>*Noun:* A slender roasting rod. |

#### Irregular Conjugations
These verbs undergo an internal vowel shift or remain static in their past tense.

| Inglisce Verb | Conjugations | Derived Noun | Definitions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to **pyt** | pyt, pyts, pyt, pytting | **pytte**, **pyts** *(pl.)* | *Verb:* To set into opposition.<br>*Noun:* A deep hole or arena. |
| to **spyt** | spyt, spyts, spat, spytting | **spytte**, **spyttle** | *Verb:* To forcibly eject saliva.<br>*Noun:* The saliva itself. |
| to **fit** | fit, fits, fit, fitting | **fitte**, **fits** *(pl.)* | *Verb:* To be the correct size/shape.<br>*Noun:* A seizure; a tailored shape. |
| to **rid** | rid, rids, rid, ridding | **riddance** | *Verb:* To eliminate.<br>*Noun:* The act of discarding. |
| to **scit** | scit, scits, sciat, sciting | **scite** | *Verb:* To defecate.<br>*Noun:* Feces. |
| to **bid** | bid, bids, bâde, bidding | **bidde**, **bids** *(pl.)* | *Verb:* To issue a command or price.<br>*Noun:* An offer of a price. |

### 2. Polysyllables (The Romance Base)

When multisyllabic Latin and French imports derive into nouns, the spelling of the base verb is dictated by its phonetic stress and its etymological root. 

#### Unstressed Roots (The Lean Verb)
If the spoken stress falls on the beginning of the word, the terminal `/ɪ/` remains unstressed. These verbs remain strictly lean. They derive into nouns using standard complex suffixes.

| Inglisce Verb | Conjugations | Derived Noun | Orthographic Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to **èdit** | èdit, èdits, èdited, èditing | **èdit**, **edicion** | Uses grave `è` to anchor initial stress. |
| to **limit** | limit, limits, limited, limiting | **limit**, **limitâcion** | Unstressed terminal remains lean. |

#### Stressed Roots (The Heavy Verb)
If the spoken stress falls heavily on the final syllable (e.g., ad-MIT), and the word derives from the Latin *mittere*, the verb structurally requires the heavy **-mitte** root. This explicitly marks them as a specific verb class, making their transition into `-mission` nouns highly predictable. 

*Note: In cases of zero-derivation within this class (such as permit), the noun shifts the spoken stress to the first syllable, which is marked with the circumflex.*

| Inglisce Verb | Conjugations (Standard) | Derived Noun | 
| :--- | :--- | :--- | 
| to **admitte** | admitte, -s, -d, -ing | **admission** | 
| to **comitte** | comitte, -s, -d, -ing | **comission** |
| to **emitte** | emitte, -s, -d, -ing | **emission** | 
| to **sobmitte** | sobmitte, -s, -d, -ing | **sobmission** | 
| to **remitte** | remitte, -s, -d, -ing | **remission** | 
| to **omitte** | omitte, -s, -d, -ing | **omission** | 
| to **permitte** | permitte, -s, -d, -ing | **permission**, **pêrmit** |
