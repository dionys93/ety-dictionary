## Pluralization of ⟨-fe⟩ Nouns

### Overview

Inglisce **eliminates the ⟨-f → -ves⟩ alternation** found in Modern English.

Nouns ending in ⟨-fe⟩ form their plural **regularly with ⟨-fs⟩**, preserving the written ⟨f⟩ in all forms.

This removes a morphologically irregular plural class while maintaining natural pronunciation.

---

### Core Rule

- **Singular:** base form  
- **Plural:** drop final ⟨-e⟩, add ⟨-s⟩  
- No consonant mutation occurs in orthography

Although written ⟨-fs⟩, the cluster is **regularly voiced in speech** as /vz/ where phonotactically expected.

(See **stem-voicing.md**.)

---

### Examples

| Modern English | Inglisce Singular | Inglisce Plural |
|---------------|------------------|-----------------|
| leaf / leaves | liefe | liefs |
| wife / wives | uîfe | uîfs |
| knife / knives | nîfe | nîfs |
| wolf / wolves | uôlfe | uôlfs |
| hoof / hooves | hôfe | hôfs |
| calf / calves | càfe | càfs |
| loaf / loaves | lofe | loafs |

The written stem remains unchanged across number.

---

### Words Without Voicing

Some ⟨-fe⟩ nouns **do not voice** the cluster in speech and remain /fs/.

These behave identically in spelling.

Examples:

- beliefe → beliefs  
- chiefe → chiefs  
- proufe → proufs  

No distinction is required in orthography.

---

### Design Rationale

This treatment:

- Removes an irregular plural class  
- Preserves a single visible base across inflection  
- Aligns with Inglisce principles of **morphological transparency**  
- Allows phonology to resolve voicing without spelling alternation

Plural formation is therefore **fully regular**.

---

## Stem Voicing Before Plural ⟨-s⟩

### Overview

Inglisce does **not alter spelling to mark voicing** triggered by plural ⟨-s⟩.

As in spoken Modern English, the plural suffix ⟨-s⟩ is realized phonetically as **/s/ or /z/** depending on its phonological environment.
This behavior is **phonetic, not morphological**, and is not represented in orthography.

---

### Voicing of Plural ⟨-s⟩

The plural suffix ⟨-s⟩ is pronounced:

- **/s/** after voiceless segments  
- **/z/** after voiced segments and vowels  

This reflects ordinary Modern English phonology and applies uniformly across Inglisce plural classes.

No spelling distinction is made.

---

### Effect on Stem Consonants

When plural ⟨-s⟩ is realized as /z/, preceding consonants may be **phonetically voiced** in connected speech.

A common case is ⟨f⟩:

- written ⟨-fs⟩ may be pronounced /vz/

This voicing is:
- predictable
- non-contrastive
- resolved entirely in pronunciation

No consonant alternation is marked in spelling.

---

### Scope

This behavior applies to:

- regular ⟨-s⟩ plurals  
- ⟨-fe⟩ plurals (see *f-pluralization.md*)  
- all plural formations ending in ⟨-s⟩

Lexical exceptions exist, as in Modern English, but require no orthographic distinction.

---

### Orthographic Principle

Inglisce preserves **morphological stability** and **base transparency**.

Phonetic voicing associated with plural ⟨-s⟩ follows spoken English and is not encoded in the written form.
