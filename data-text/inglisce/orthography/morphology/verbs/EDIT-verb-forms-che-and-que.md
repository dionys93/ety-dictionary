# Nouns and Verbs in Inglisce: The Hard /k/ Paradigm (-che, -que, -c, -x)

In Modern English, the hard /k/ sound at the end of words is spelled inconsistently (e.g., *bake, bark, fake*). Furthermore, adding suffixes often creates confusing visual clusters. **Inglisce** resolves this by strictly categorizing these words using the **-che** and **-que** digraphs for verbs, and the hard **-c** for nouns.

This system creates a highly predictable visual syntax where the spelling of a word shifts slightly to indicate its exact grammatical role.

### Grammatical Mechanics

**1. The Verb Infinitive (-che / -que)**
Verbs ending in a hard /k/ sound utilize the digraphs **-che** or **-que** in their infinitive form. They retain this digraph for the past tense (**-ed**) and gerund/participle (**-ing**).
* *to bake* → **to bâche**
* *baking* → **bâching**

**2. The Noun & 1st-Person Shift (-c)**
When the word functions as a standalone noun, or when conjugated in the 1st-person present tense, the silent terminal 'e' and the digraph are dropped, reverting to a hard **-c**. This instantly signals to the reader that the word is acting as a root noun or a direct action.
* *a bake* → **a bâc**
* *I bake* → **I bâc**

**3. The Elegant Plural (-x)**
In Modern English, adding a plural 's' to a 'k' creates a `/ks/` sound that is visually messy (e.g., *bakes, barks*). Inglisce elegantly resolves this by replacing the `-cs` cluster with the single letter **-x**. 
* *bark (sing.)* → **barc**
* *barks (plur.)* → **barx**

**4. The Pre-X Vowel Mutation (â → ai)**
When forming the **-x** plural for words utilizing the **â** diacritic, the vowel mutates into the **ai** digraph. This maintains the long vowel sound visually while preparing for the consonant cluster.
* *the bake* → **bâc**
* *the bakes* → **baix**

### The Conjugation Model: "To Bâche" (To Bake)
Here is the standard conjugation paradigm for a regular **-che** verb:

| Pronoun | Present Conjugation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **I** | bâc |
| **Uie** | bâche |
| **Hie, sie** | bâcs |
| **Ћey** | bâche |
| **You** | bâc / bâche |

* **Past Tense:** bâched
* **Gerund:** bâching
* **Noun (Singular / Plural):** bâc / baix
