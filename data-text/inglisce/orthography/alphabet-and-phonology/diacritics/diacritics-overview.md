# Diacritical Marks in Inglisce

Inglisce uses five main diacritical marks (plus the diaeresis) to indicate pronunciation and stress patterns. These marks help create a more phonetic and consistent orthography while preserving etymological visual cues.

The system creates visual consistency: related sounds use related marks, making patterns easier to learn and remember.

### General Principles

**1. Stress Marking**
* Grave (`̀`) and Acute (`́`) marks explicitly indicate stressed syllables.
* In compound words, Circumflexes (`̂`) may appear without carrying the primary stress.

**2. The One-Mark Rule**
* Generally, only one diacritical mark should appear per word.

**3. Conservation**
* Use diacritical marks *only* when necessary for phonetic clarity. 
* Regular, predictable spelling patterns do not require marks.

### Teaching Notes Reference
* **Grave & Acute:** Represent stress and vowel quality.
* **Circumflex:** Represents vowel quality, but usually carries stress as well.
* **Tilde:** Represents palatalization (the addition of a /j/ glide).
* **Cedilla / Comma:** Modifies consonants (softening C/S, affricating D/T).
