# OU

The digraph **OU** represents multiple values in Inglisce, determined by environment and morphology.

**Related forms:** OUR  
**Diacritic variant:** OÛ

---

## Pronunciation Summary

| Spelling | IPA | Environment | Example |
|----------|-----|-----------------------------|---------|
| OU | /u/ | Default value | moon → **moune** |
| OUR | /ər/ | Before R | journey → **journie** |
| OU + vowel | /w/ | Before another vowel | wet → **ouet** |

---

OU regularly represents /u/.

---

## OUR → /ər/

When followed by **R**, OU represents /ər/.

| Modern English | Inglisce |
|----------------|----------|
| nurture | nourțure |
| journey | journie |
| sojourn | sojourne |

---

## OU + Vowel → /w/

Before another vowel, OU represents /w/.

| Modern English | Inglisce |
|----------------|----------|
| where | ouere |
| wet | ouet |

---

# OÛ

The digraph **OÛ** represents /ʊ/ in a restricted morphological environment.

---

## Pronunciation Summary

| Spelling | IPA | Environment | Example |
|----------|-----|----------------|---------|
| OÛ | /ʊ/ | Modal verbs | could → **coûd** |

---

## Modal Verb Rule

In modal verbs, /ʊ/ is written **OÛ**.

### Examples

| Modern English | Inglisce |
|----------------|----------|
| could | coûd |
| would | oûd |

## Teaching Notes

### Primary Patterns

- OU → /u/ by default  
- OUR → /ər/  
- OU + vowel → /w/  

### Key Distinction

- **OÛ** is restricted to modal verbs and always represents /ʊ/.
