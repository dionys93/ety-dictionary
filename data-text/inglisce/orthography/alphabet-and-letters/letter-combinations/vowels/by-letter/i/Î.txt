Î

PRONUNCIATION
The letter Î represents one sound:
• /aɪ/ — as in "find" (usually stressed)

RULES

1. General Use
Î represents /aɪ/ in various positions.

Examples:
    to combine → to combîne
    isle → île
    vile → vîle
    violence → vîlence
    to violate → to vîolete
    idea → îdía
    irony → îronie
    to contrive → to contrîve
    to abide → to abîde
    to find → to fînd
    to grind → to grînd
    divine → divîne
    knight → nîht
    high → hîh
    height → hîht

2. Latin Plurals
-Î forms plurals of some Latin-derived words.

Examples:
    octopus → octopî
    alumnus → alomnî
    fungus → fongî

TEACHING NOTES
• Î = /aɪ/ (like "eye")
• Usually stressed but not always (e.g., îdía has stress on í)
• Used for Latin plurals ending in -us → -î
