# IE

The digraph IE represents one vowel sound in Inglisce.

### PRONUNCIATION SUMMARY

- **IE** — /i/ "field" — field → fielde

### The Digraph IE

#### IE = /i/
IE consistently represents /i/ in all positions.

### Verb Patterns

#### Verb Endings
IE appears at the end of verbs.

**Examples:**  
to envy → to envie  
to worry → to uêrie  
to hurry → to hêrie  
to marry → to mârie

#### Mid-Word Position
IE appears in the middle of some verbs.

**Examples:**  
to feel → to fiele  
to deal → to diele  
to kneel → to niele  
to meet → to miete

### Noun Patterns

#### Singular Ending -IE
Nouns ending in -Y in Modern English use -IE in the singular.

**Pattern:** -IE singular → -IS plural

**Examples:**  
enemy → enemie, enemis  
community → comunetie, comunetis  
policy → pólicie, pólicis

### Teaching Notes

**Primary patterns:**
- IE always = /i/
- No pronunciation variation

**Key distinctions:**
- Common in verb forms (both endings and mid-word)
- -IE nouns → -IS plurals
- Replaces Modern English -Y in singular nouns
