# OA

The digraph **OA** represents a **single vowel spelling class** in Inglisce, with a **primary value /oʊ/** and a **restricted secondary value /aʊ/** in specific environments.

---

## Pronunciation Summary

### Primary Value

| Spelling | IPA | Example (Modern English) | Inglisce (sg) | Inglisce (pl) |
|--------|-----|--------------------------|---------------|---------------|
| OA | /oʊ/ | *boat* | **bote** | **boats** |

---

### Restricted Secondary Value

In a small, closed set of words, **OA** represents **/aʊ/** before historical *-ch*.

| Modern English | IPA | Inglisce |
|---------------|-----|----------|
| couch | /kaʊtʃ/ | **coac̃e** |
| pouch | /paʊtʃ/ | **poac̃e** |
| vouch | /vaʊtʃ/ | **voac̃e** |

This pattern is **non-productive** and does not extend to new formations.

## Morphological Notes

- **Singular nouns** typically end in **-E**
- **Plural nouns** retain **OA** and add **-S**
- OA is **orthographic**, not phonemic transcription

---

## Teaching Notes

### Primary Pattern

- **OA consistently represents /oʊ/**
- Morphology governs alternation:  
  **-E (sg) ↔ OA + S (pl)**

### Key Distinction

- OA often replaces **O + silent E** while maintaining Romance-style visual regularity
- The /aʊ/ value is **lexically restricted** and should be taught as an exception class
