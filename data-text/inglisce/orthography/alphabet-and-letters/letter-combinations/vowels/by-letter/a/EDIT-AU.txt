AU

PRONUNCIATION
The digraph AU represents two sounds:
/ɔ/ — as in "lawn" (most common)
/ɔr/ — when followed by R
/æʊ/

RULES

1. Basic AU = /ɔ/
In most contexts, AU is pronounced /ɔ/.

Examples:
    Australia → Austrâlha /ɔˈstreɪljə/
    autumn → automn /ˈɔɾəm/
    automatic → automatic /ˌɔɾəˈmæɾɪk/
    lawn → laun /lɔn/
    dawn → daun /dɔn/
    pawn → paun /pɔn/
    coffee → caufie /'kɔfi/

2. Verb/Noun Distinction
Verbs use -AUHE while related nouns use -AƜ.

Examples:
    to saw → ta sauhe /tə sɔ/
    the saw → þe saɯ /ðə sɔ/
    
    to draw → ta drauhe /tə drɔ/
    the draw → þe draɯ /ðə drɔ/

3. AU + R = /ɔr/
When AU is followed by R, it's pronounced /ɔr/.

Unstressed examples:
    war → ɯaur /wɔr/
    roar → raur /rɔr/
    boar → baur /bɔr/
    oar → aur /ɔr/

Stressed examples (written as AÛ):
    reward → reɯaûd /rɪˈwɔrd/
    warden → ɯaûdan /ˈwɔrdən/
    warn → ɯaûn /wɔrn/
    warm → ɯaûm /wɔrm/
    quarrel → cuaûle /ˈkwɔrəl/

TEACHING NOTES
• Basic AU = /ɔ/
• AU + R = /ɔr/ (unstressed: AUR, stressed: AÛ)
• Verbs use -AUHE, nouns use -AƜ
