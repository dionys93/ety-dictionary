# IPA /aɪər/ — "FIRE" DIPHTHONG

## Sound Overview

| Feature | Value |
|----------|--------|
| IPA | /aɪər/ |
| Description | Low-front to high-front closing diphthong + rhotacization |
| Example in Modern English | fire, hire, desire |
| Frequency | Moderately common; especially in Latinate verb stems |

---

## Spelling Patterns

| Category | Spellings |
|-----------|------------|
| Primary | Î (in ÎRE stems) |
| Noun plural | -ÎARS |
| Verb infinitive | -ÎER |

'*' Represents no change in spelling between Modern (Websterian) English and Inglisce

## Core Distribution Rules

### 1. ÎRE = /aɪər/ (Nouns)

The default spelling for /aɪər/ in nouns. Plurals follow the -ÎARS pattern.

| Modern | Inglisce |

### 3. Derived Nouns

Verbs with /aɪər/ stems form derived nouns via Latin/French endings, often losing the diphthong.

| Inglisce Verb | Inglisce Noun |
