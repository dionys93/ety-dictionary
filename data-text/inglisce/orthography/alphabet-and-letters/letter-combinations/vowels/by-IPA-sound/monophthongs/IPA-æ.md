# IPA /æ/ — "CAT" VOWEL

## Sound Overview

| Feature | Value |
|----------|--------|
| IPA | /æ/ |
| Description | Near-low near-front unrounded vowel |
| Example in Modern English | cat, bat, analysis |
| Frequency | Very common; primary stressed A sound in Inglisce |

---

## Spelling Patterns

| Category | Spellings |
|-----------|------------|
| Primary | A (stressed, single vowel) |
| Stress-marked | À (irregular stress patterns) |

'*' Represents no change in spelling between Modern (Websterian) English and Inglisce

### 2. À = /æ/ (Irregular Stress)

Grave accent marks stressed /æ/ where stress is not predictable from spelling alone.

| Modern | Inglisce |
|--------|----------|
| analysis | anàlysis |
| combatant | combàtant |
| alfalfa | alfàlfa |

---

### 3. Variable Stress Patterns

Some roots shift stress — and spelling — depending on part of speech.

| Modern | Inglisce | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| animate (verb) | animait | /ˈænɪˌmeɪt/ |
| animate (adjective) | animat | /ˈænɪmət/ |
| practical | * | base form |
| practicality | practicaletie | derived form |
