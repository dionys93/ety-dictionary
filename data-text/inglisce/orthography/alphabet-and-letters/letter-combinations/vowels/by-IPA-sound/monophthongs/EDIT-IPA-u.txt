IPA /u/ — "MOON" VOWEL

SOUND OVERVIEW
- IPA symbol: /u/
- Description: High-back rounded vowel
- Audio equivalent: as in "moon", "blue", "food"
- Frequency: Common in Inglisce, appears in multiple spelling patterns

SPELLING PATTERNS
- Primary: OU (consistent), U (before silent E), UE (word-final)
- Stress-marked: Ú (stressed)
- Secondary: EU (after R, TR)

DETAILED RULES

1. OU = /u/ (Consistent)
   soup, soups -> soupe, soups 
   route, routes -> route, routs
   fool, fools → foule, fouls 
   stool, stools → stoule, stouls 
   room, rooms → roume, roums
   soon → soun (adv), 
   moon, moons → moune, mouns 
   noon → noune
   boom, booms → boume, boums 
   loot → loute, 
   tomb, tombs → toume, toums
   coop, coops → coupe, coups 
   stoop, stoops → stoupe, stoups
   tube, tubes → toube, toubs
   school, schools → scoule, scouls

2. U + Silent E = /ju/
   cute, mute, muse
   R + U + Silent E = /u/
   rude, crude

   - Before vowels: nuance, congruence, influence /u/

3. UE = /u/ (Word-Final)
   blue, clue, 
   due → dûe, dûs
   to sue → to sue -s -d suing
   view → vue, vuis
   hue → hue, huis
   avenue

5. EU = /u/ (After R, TR)
   - After R, TR: rule → reule, true → treu, truth → treuth
   - Limited to specific consonant contexts
