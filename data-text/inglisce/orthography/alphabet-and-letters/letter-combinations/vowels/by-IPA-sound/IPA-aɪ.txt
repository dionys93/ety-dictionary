# IPA /aɪ/ — "EYE" DIPHTHONG

SOUND OVERVIEW
- IPA symbol: /aɪ/
- Description: Low-front to high-front closing diphthong
- Audio equivalent: as in "eye", "find", "guy", "sky"
- Frequency: Common in Inglish, appears in multiple contexts

SPELLING PATTERNS
- Primary: Î (most common), YH (specific words)
- Secondary: AE (Latin -fy verbs), ÁI (rare), UY (very rare)
- Contextual: YH for traditional English words, AE for Latin derivatives

DETAILED RULES

1. Î = /aɪ/ (Main Pattern)
   - Find verbs: to find → to fînd, to grind → to grînd, to bind → to bînd
   - Combine family: to combine → to combîne, to contrive → to contrîve
   - Abide: to abide → to abîde, to reside → to resîde
   - Adjectives: divine → divîne, vile → vîle, fine → fîne
   - Nouns: isle → île, violence → vîlence, idea → îdía
   - Light family: high → hîh, knight → nîht, height → hîht, light → lîht

2. YH = /aɪ/ (Traditional English Words)
   - Lie verbs: to lie → to lyhe (tell falsehood), to dye → to dyhe
   - Motion: to fly → to flyhe
   - Nouns: sky → skyh, rye → ryh
   - Commerce: to buy → to byhe, to tie → to tyhe
   - Emotions: to sigh → to syhe, to cry → to crîe
   - Adjectives: tight → tyht, shy → shyh, bright → bryht

3. AE = /aɪ/ (Latin -fy Verbs)
   - Justify family: to justify → to justifae, to clarify → to clârifae
   - Amplify: to amplify → to amplifae, to simplify → to simplifae
   - Verify: to verify → to verifae, to modify → to modifae
   - Pattern: Latin -ficare → French -fier → English -fy → Inglish -fae

4. ÁI = /aɪ/ (Rare/Exceptional)
   - Aisle: aisle → áile
   - Night: night → náit 
   - Very limited vocabulary

5. UY = /aɪ/ (Very Rare)
   - E.g. guy, guys
   - Extremely limited use

6. Î Latin Plurals
   - Octopus: octopus → octopî
   - Alumnus: alomnus → alomnî
   - Fungus: fongus → fongî
   - Pattern: Latin -us → -î plurals

EXAMPLES BY CATEGORY
- Î verbs: fînd, grînd, bînd, combîne, contrîve, abîde, resîde, crîe, trîe
- Î adjectives/nouns: divîne, vîle, fîne, île, vîlence, îdía
- Î light family: hîh, nîht, hîht, lîht
- YH traditional words: lyhe, dyhe, flyhe, skyh, ryh, byhe, tyhe, syhe
- YH adjectives: tyht, shyh
- AE Latin verbs: justifae, clarifae, amplifae, simplifae, verifae, modifae
- Rare forms: áile, náit, guy/guys
- Latin plurals: octopî, alomnî, fongî

CROSS-REFERENCES
- Related sounds: /aɪər/ (see IPA-aɪər when +R)
- Etymology: AE from Latin -ficare, YH from Old English
- Alternative patterns: Î (general) vs YH (traditional) vs AE (Latin)
- See also: Î.txt, Y.txt, AE.txt, ÁI.txt, UY.txt

TEACHING NOTES
- Î is the default spelling for /aɪ/ in most contexts
- YH preserves traditional English words (fly, sky, buy, etc.)
- AE specifically for Latin-derived -fy verbs → -fae
- Latin plurals use Î (octopî, not octopuses)
- ÁI and UY are rare exceptions for specific words
- Pattern recognition: science/formal = Î, traditional = YH, Latin verbs = AE
