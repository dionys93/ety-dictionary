IPA /æ/ — "CAT" VOWEL

SOUND OVERVIEW
- IPA symbol: /æ/
- Description: Near-low near-front unrounded vowel
- Audio equivalent: as in "cat", "bat", "analysis"
- Frequency: Very common in Inglish, primary stressed A sound

SPELLING PATTERNS
- Primary: A (stressed, single vowel)
- Stress-marked: À (irregular stress patterns)

DETAILED RULES

1. A = /æ/ (Stressed Single Vowel)
   - Monosyllables: cat, hat, fat, sat, bat, mat
   - Simple words: rap, tap, map, gap, cap, lap
   - Before consonants: most common pattern for stressed A

2. À = /æ/ (Irregular Stress)
   - Stress clarification: analysis → anàlysis
   - Borrowed words: combatant → combàtant, alfalfa → alfàlfa
   - Etymology corrections: admiral → àmiral (removes unetymological -d-)

3. Variable Stress Patterns
   - Same root, different stress: 
     - animate (verb) → to animait /tə ˈænɪˌmeɪt/
     - animate (adjective) → animat /ˈænɪmət/
   - Derivatives: practical, practicality → practicaletie

EXAMPLES BY CATEGORY
- Simple monosyllables: cat, hat, fat, sat, bat, mat, rap, tap, map, gap
- Irregular stress À: anàlysis, combàtant, alfàlfa, àmiral
- Variable patterns: animait/animat, practìcal/practicaletie

CROSS-REFERENCES
- Related sounds: /ɑ/ (see IPA-ɑ), /ə/ (see IPA-ə when unstressed)
- Stress variants: A (regular) ↔ À (irregular stress)
- Unstressed becomes: /ə/ (see variable patterns)
- See also: A.txt, À.txt

TEACHING NOTES
- A as single stressed vowel almost always = /æ/
- Use À when stress isn't obvious from spelling patterns
- Watch for stress shifts in verb/adjective pairs
- Key contrast: /æ/ vs /ɑ/ ("cat" vs "cot")
- Most common vowel sound in stressed A
