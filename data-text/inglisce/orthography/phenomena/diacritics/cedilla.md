## The Cedilla in Inglisce

### Overview

In Inglisce, the **cedilla** (or **comma below**, where font support requires it) is used to represent specific consonant sounds consistently and transparently.

Cedillas may appear only on the letters **c, d, s, t** and **x**.

Their primary purpose is to preserve **etymological continuity** while clearly marking **predictable sound changes** that arise in English derivation.

---

### Function and Scope

Cedillas in Inglisce are used to:

- Preserve relationships between base forms and their derivatives  
- Mark consonant softening and affrication systematically  
- Reduce ambiguity in environments where English spelling is inconsistent  

They do **not** indicate stress, vowel quality, or syllable structure.

---

## ç — Soft C

### Pronunciation

| Environment | IPA |
|------------|-----|
| Before **a, o, u** | /s/ |
| Before **e, i** | /ʃ/ |

### Examples

**/s/ before a, o, u**
| English | Inglisce |
|------------|-----|
| façade | **façade** |
| juice, juicer | **juce**, **juçor** |
| slice, slicer, sliceable | **slîce**, **slîçor**, **slîçable** |
| space, spacer | **spâce**, **spâçor** |

**/ʃ/ before e, i**
| English | Inglisce |
|------------|-----|
| appreciate, appreciation | **apríçiait**, **apriçiâcion** |
| associate, association | **assoçiait**, **assoçiâcion** |
| negociate, negociation | **negoçiait**, **negoçiâcion** |
| efficient | **effiçent** |
| omniscent | **omniçent** |
| proficient | **profiçent** |
| publish | **publiçe** |
| suffice, sufficient | **sofîce**, **sofiçent** |

## ḑ — Soft D

### Pronunciation

| Letter | IPA |
|------|-----|
| ḑ | /ʤ/ |

### Examples

| English | Inglisce |
|------|-----|
| educate, education | **eḑucait**, **eḑucâcion** |
| graduate, graducation | **graḑuait**, **graḑuâcion** |
| proceed, procedure | **procide**, **procíḑure** |

The forms **d̦** and **ḑ** are orthographically equivalent and may be used interchangeably depending on font support.

## ș — Soft S

### Pronunciation

| Letter | IPA |
|------|-----|
| ș | /ʃ/ |

### Distribution

- Most commonly appears in words derived from **French stems ending in -iss**
- Also found in certain **Germanic words**, typically irregularly

### Examples

| English | Inglisce |
|------------|-----|
| abolish | **abolișe** |
| finish | **finișe** |
| vanish | **vanișe**  |
| shut | **șote** |

## ț — Soft T

### Pronunciation and Environment

| Environment | IPA |
|------------|-----|
| Between two vowels | /ʧ/ |
| In **xț**, **nț + vowel** | /ʧ/ |
| After **consonant + vowel** | /ʃ/ |

### Examples

**/ʧ/**
| English | Inglisce |
|------------|-----|
| denture | **dențure** |
| feature | **fiețure** |
| mixture | **mixțure** |
| nature, natural | **nâțure**, **națural** | 

**/ʃ/**
| English | Inglisce |
|------------|-----|
| actual |  **acțual** |
| capture | **capțure** |
| effectual | **effecțual** |
| factual | **facțual** |
| intellectual | **intellecțual** |
| picture | **picțure** |

---

### Orthographic Contrast

- Cedillas are **not optional stylistic markers**; they encode phonological information.
- Unmarked **c, d, s, t** retain their default values.
- Cedillas allow Inglisce to maintain Romance-style **derivational transparency** without sacrificing phonetic clarity. 

See [Diacritics Overview](diacritics-overview.md) for a comparison with vowel and consonantal diacritics.

---

### Summary

Cedillas in Inglisce serve to:

1. Preserve etymological relationships between word families  
2. Indicate consonant shifts that arise during English derivation  
3. Reduce spelling ambiguity through consistent sound marking  

This system maintains visible links between base forms (*juce, spâce, slîce, acte*) and their derivatives, while clearly encoding the phonological changes that occur.
