# Diacritics in Inglisce

### Overview

In Inglisce, diacritics serve to **clarify pronunciation, stress, and consonant structure**.  
They are divided into three functional categories:

1. **Stress and vowel quality:** Acute (´), Grave (`)  
2. **Consonant clusters:** Tilde (˜)  
3. **Other:** Diaeresis (¨)

---

### Functional Comparison

| Diacritic | Function | Examples | Notes |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| Acute (´) | Marks non-default stress; preserves vowel quality | fáþre, contéinment | Only one accent per word |
| Grave (`) | Marks non-default stress; preserves vowel quality | anàlysis, regrèts | Same rules as acute; complements vowel contexts |
| Tilde (˜) | Marks /mj/ and /nj/ clusters | am̃olet, conviñent | Indicates nasal + yod unit; interacts with vowel reduction |

---

### Rules and Constraints

- Only one accent (acute, grave, or circumflex) per word; diaeresis may co-occur.  
- Diacritics are used when default stress or vowel quality rules are insufficient.  
- Tilde never replaces vowel accents; it marks consonant sequences.
