Latin-derived -atus Adjectives

Many Latin verbs formed perfect participles ending in -atus. 
In Inglisce, adjectives derived from these forms follow systematic patterns that maintain their etymological connections.


Pattern A: -âte → -atif
to relâte → relatif
to negâte → negatif


Pattern B: -de → -ssif
to decîde → decîssif
to explode → explóssif
to erode → eróssif
to corrode → corróssif


Pattern C: -cte → -ctif
to acte → actif
to infecte → infectif
to protecte → protectif


Pattern D: -ait → -atif
to demonstrait → demonstratif
to opperaite → opratif
to cooperait → coöpratif


Pattern E: -mitte → -missif
to admitte → admissif
to permitte → permissif
to sobmitte → sobmissif


Pattern F: -âte → -âtif
to criâte → criâtif
to equâte → equâtif


Pronunciation:
The -if ending is pronounced /ɪv/, like English -ive
