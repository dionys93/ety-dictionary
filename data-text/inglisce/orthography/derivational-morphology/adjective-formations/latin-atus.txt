Latin-derived -atus Adjectives

Many Latin verbs formed perfect participles ending in -atus. 
In Inglisce, adjectives derived from these forms follow systematic patterns that maintain their etymological connections.


Pattern A: -âte → -atif
to relâte → relatif
to negâte → negatif


Pattern B: -de → -ssif
to decîde → decîssif
to explode → explôssif
to erode → erôssif
to corode → corôssif


Pattern C: -cte → -ctif
to acte → actif
to infecte → infèctif
to protecte → protèctif


Pattern D: -ete/-aite → -aitif/-atif
to demonstrait → demonstraitif
to óperaite → ópratif
to cuóperaite → coópratif
to m̃utait → m̃utaitif
to pêmiait → pêmiaitif


Pattern E: -mitte → -missif
to admitte → admissif
to permitte → permissif
ta submitte → submissif


Irregular Formations:
to criâte → criâtif
to equâte → equâtif


Pronunciation:
The -if ending is pronounced /ɪv/, like English -ive
