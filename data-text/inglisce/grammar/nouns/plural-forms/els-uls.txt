Plural Formation for Nouns Ending in -le

Nouns ending in -le form their plurals by changing -le to either -uls or -els, depending on their etymology.

1. Change -le to -uls
   circle → circuls (Latin circulus)
   câble → câbuls (Latin capulus, capulum)
   cîcle → cîculs (Latin cyclus)
   modle → moduls (Latin modulus)
   musçle → musçuls (Latin musculus)
   tâble → tâbuls (Latin tabula)
   ângle → ânguls (Latin angulus)
   ígle → íguls (Latin aquilus)
   îcicle → îciculs (-uls because -c- is a voiceless velar stop, also for conformity)

2. Change -le to -els
   cassle → cassels (Latin castellum)
   mussle → mussels
   reble → rebels (Latin rebellis)
   tonle → tonels (Old French tonel)
