# Prepositions

As a closed grammatical class, prepositions in Inglisce function syntactically exactly as they do in Modern English. They establish spatial, temporal, and logical relationships within a sentence. 

While their orthography has been standardized to reflect their phonetic reality, their usage, deployment, and meaning remain completely unchanged.

### Core Preposition Matrix

| Modern English | Inglisce | Definition / Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **about** | **aboûte** | Concerning a subject; located around or nearby. |
| **above** | **abovfe** | In extended space over and not touching; at a higher level. |
| **across** | **acrosse** | From one side to the other of a space, area, or line. |
| **after** | **aftre** | In the time following an event; behind in order or sequence. |
| **against** | **againste** | In opposition to; in physical contact or collision with. |
| **along** | **alonge** | Moving in a constant direction on a path or line. |
| **around** | **aroûnde** | Located or situated on every side; enclosing. |
| **at** | **ate** | Expressing a specific location, time, or target. |
| **before** | **before** | During the period of time preceding an event; in front of. |
| **behind** | **behînde** | At or to the far side of something; in the rear. |
| **below** | **beloe** | At a lower level or layer than something else. |
| **beneath** | **beniaþe** | Extending or directly underneath, often carrying a formal tone. |
| **beside** | **besîde** | At the side of; next to. |
| **between** | **betuiene** | At, into, or across the space separating two distinct things. |
| **beyond** | **beyonde** | At or to the further side of; outside the physical limits of. |
| **by** | **by** | Identifying the agent of an action; indicating proximity (near to). |
| **down** | **dôun** | Toward or in a lower place or position. |
| **for** | **for** | In support of; intended to belong to or benefit someone/something. |
| **from** | **from** | Indicating the point in space or time where a journey or process begins. |
| **in** | **in** | Expressing the state of being enclosed or surrounded by something. |
| **inside** | **insîde** | Situated strictly within the boundaries of a container or space. |
| **into** | **intou** | Expressing movement or direction resulting in enclosure. |
| **near** | **niare** | At or to a short distance away in space or time. |
| **of** | **of** | Expressing the relationship between a part and a whole; origin. |
| **off** | **offe** | Moving away and often down from; indicating detachment. |
| **on** | **on** | Physically in contact with and supported by a surface. |
| **out** | **oûte** | Moving or appearing to move away from a particular place. |
| **over** | **ovre** | Extending directly upward from; expressing passage across. |
| **through** | **þru** | Moving in one side and out the other side of an opening, channel, or location. |
| **to** | **tou** | Expressing motion in the direction of a particular location; identifying a target. |
| **under** | **ondre** | Extending or directly below something; controlled or governed by. |
| **until** | **ontil** | Up to the point in time or the event mentioned. |
| **up** | **oppe** | Toward a higher place or position. |
| **with** | **uiþ** | Accompanied by; possessing or having. |
| **within** | **uiþin** | Occurring inside the range, limits, or boundaries of. |
| **without** | **uiþoûte** | In the absence of; not having or doing. |
