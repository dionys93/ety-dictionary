# Regular Verb Conjugation

Regular verbs in Inglisce conjugate predictably across three main inflectional combinations. For our example, we will use the regular verb **to c̃aice** (Modern English: *to chase*).

### 1. 3rd Person Singular (Present Tense)
Add **-s** to the base form.
* **Sie c̃aices þe cate.** (She chases the cat.)

### 2. The Past Tense
Add **-d** (or **-ed**) to the base form. This form remains uniform across all pronouns.
* **I c̃aiced þe cate.** (I chased the cat.)
* **You c̃aiced þe cate.** (You chased the cat.)
* **Sie c̃aiced þe cate.** (She chased the cat.)

### 3. Progressive Tenses
Drop the terminal silent **-e** and add **-ing**.
* **Sie's c̃aicing þe cate.** (She is chasing the cat.)
* **Ћey uere c̃aicing þe cate.** (They were chasing the cat.)
