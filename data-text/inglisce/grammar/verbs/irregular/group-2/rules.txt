GROUP 2 IRREGULAR VERBS: CONJUGATION PATTERNS


Verbs with Different Forms for Transitive vs. Intransitive Uses
Some verbs have different forms depending on whether they're used transitively or intransitively:

Examples:
hângue hâng(s hong -ing (intransitive)
hângue hâng(s -d -ing (transitive)

criepe -s craipt -ing (intransitive)
criepe -s -d -ing (transitive/intransitive)

bêne -s bênt -ing (intransitive)
bêne -s -d -ing (transitive)

smelle -s smelt -ing (intransitive)
smelle -s -d -ing (transitive)

spuile -s spuilt spilling (intransitive)
spuile -s -d spilling (transitive)


Some verbs change both their vowel and ending in past tense:
Examples:
  telle → tôld
  selle → sôld
  scîne → sione -ing
  sioute → siat -ing
