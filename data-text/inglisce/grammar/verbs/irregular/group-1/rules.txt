Pattern 1: E-Droppers

Drop final '-e' for past tense while maintaining the same consonant ending

    to buide (bid) - buide(s, buid, buiding
    to bêste (burst) - bêste(s, bêst, bêsting
    to caste (cast) - caste(s, cast, casting
    to coste (cost) - coste(s, cost, costing
    to cöte (cut) - cöte(s, cöt, cöting
    to fuite (fit) - fuite(s, fuit, fuiting
    to huite (hit) - huite(s, huit, huiting
    to hurte (hurt) - hurte(s, hurt, hurting
    to leade (lead) - leade(s, lead, leading 
    to puite (pit) - puite(s, puit, puiting
    to pûte (put) - pûte(s, pût, pûting
    to qüite (quit) - qüite(s, qüit, qüiting
    to ruide (rid) - ruide(s, ruid, ruiding
    to seute (shut) - seute(s, seut, seuting
    to spluite (split) - spluite(s, spluit, spluiting

Pattern 2 with 'e' accent in past tense:
Verbs with 'e' as main vowel add accent in past tense and progressive

    to lait (let) - lait(s, lèt, laiting
    to bete (bet) - bete(s, bèt, bèting
    to sete (set) - sete(s, sèt, sèting
    to sprede (spread) - sprede(s, sprèd, sprèding
    to uede (wed) - uede(s, uèd, uèding
