# Group 3 Irregular Verbs: The Strong Verbs (Ablaut)

Group 3 verbs represent the historical core of the Germanic strong verb system. Unlike weak verbs (which add a `-t` or `-d` to form the past tense), strong verbs change tense purely through **Ablaut**—a systematic, predictable mutation of the internal root vowel.

By observing their conjugations in Inglisce, these verbs are characterized by two major features:
1. They maintain three distinct principal parts (Present / Past / Participle).
2. Their past participles are heavily marked by an **-n** or **-an** terminal suffix.

These vowel mutations fall into distinct, highly predictable structural patterns:

### 3A. The "A/O" Nasal Shift
Verbs that feature an **m**, **n**, or **ng** in their root undergo a very consistent mutation where the past tense shifts to an **a** or **â**, and the participle rounds to an **o**.

| Infinitive | Past | Past Participle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to singue | sâng | song |
| to suimme | suam | suom |
| to rone | ran | ron |
| to come | câme | com |

### 3B. The "O" Shift with "-an" Participles
This large category shifts the internal vowel to an **o** variant in the past tense. Their past participles explicitly attach an **-an** or **-n** suffix. This group breaks down into two distinct sub-patterns based on the starting vowel:

**1. The High-Vowel Shift (î / ai → o / oa)**
For verbs starting with high vowels, the past participle reverts back to the short **i** sound, locked in by the suffix.
| Infinitive | Past | Past Participle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to drîve | droave | drivan |
| to r̃aite | r̃oat | r̃itan |

**2. The Mid-Vowel Shift (â / ei → ô / ó)**
For verbs starting with mid-vowels, the past tense shifts to **ô** or **ó**. The participle retains the **o** or reverts to the original **â**.
| Infinitive | Past | Past Participle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to tâche | tôc | tâcan |
| to breiche | bróc | brócan |
| to beire | bór | born |

*(Note: Verbs ending in the soft **-che**, such as **tâche** and **breiche**, explicitly drop the **h** to form a hard **-c** in their past and participle forms).*

### 3C. The Terminal Glide Shift (-oe → -ou / -eu)
Verbs ending in **-oe** (or the similar **-oihe**) manage historical "W" glides. They shift their terminal vowel to the tight diphthongs **-ou** or **-eu** in the past tense. Their participles are formed by adding an **-n** or **-ne** to the base vowel.

| Infinitive | Past | Past Participle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to groe | grou | grone |
| to þroe | þreu | þreon |
| to floihe | flou | flone |

### 3D. Distinct Irregulars
A few verbs feature unique vowel shifts that do not cleanly align with the broader categories above, though they still strictly utilize the **-en** or **-ne** strong participle structure.

| Infinitive | Past | Past Participle |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| to eite | éit | eiten | 
| to sihe | sah | sine |

---

### General Conjugation Rules for Strong Verbs
* **3rd Person Singular:** Add **-s** to the infinitive root (e.g., *sie drîves*, *sie þroes*). Verbs ending in soft **-che** drop the **h** to avoid clustering (e.g., *to tâche* → *sie tâc(s)*).
* **Progressive Participle:** Drop the terminal silent **-e** from the infinitive base and add **-ing** (e.g., *to drîve* → *drîving*, *to singue* → *singuing*).
