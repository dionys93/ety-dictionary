GROUP 3 IRREGULAR VERBS: CONJUGATION PATTERNS

These verbs represent the core of Germanic strong verbs, maintaining ancient ablaut (vowel gradation) patterns from Old English.
They show systematic vowel changes between present, past, and past participle forms, following predictable phonological patterns.

PATTERN A: I → Â → O ABLAUT
------------------------------
Verbs with high vowel /i/ that alternates to /a/ then /o/

drincue (drânc/dronc), sincue (sânc/sonc), rhingue (rhâng/rhong), singue (sâng/song), springue (sprâng/sprong), șrincue (șrânc/șronc), stincue (stânc/stonc)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to drincue
- Simple Present: I drinc, sie drincs, uie drincue, þey drincue, you drinc/drincue
- Simple Past: I drânc, sie drânc, uie drânc, þey drânc, you drânc
- Past Participle: (have) dronc/-ing

Subpattern A2: I → A → O ABLAUT
beguin (begane/begon), suïme (suam/suom)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to suïme
- Simple Present: I suïme, sie suïmes, uie suïme, þey suïme
- Simple Past: I suam, sie suam, uie suam, þey suam
- Past Participle: (have) suom

Key: The -cue verbs all have nasal consonants (nc, ng) in the stem. Vowel shifts from i → â in past, then to o in participle.

PATTERN B: Î → O → I ABLAUT
----------------------------
Verbs with long /î/ that becomes /o/ in past, returning to /i/ in participle

drîve (drove/drivan), rîde (rode/ridan), rîte (roat/ritan), strîde (strode/stridan), rîse (rose/risan), arîse (arose/arisan)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to drîve
- Simple Present: I drîve, sie drîves, uie drîve, þey drîve
- Simple Past: I drove, sie drove, uie drove, þey drove
- Past Participle: (have) drivan

Subpattern B2 - participle with -an, past keeps /i/:
bîte (bit/bitan), hîde (hid/hidan)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to bîte
- Simple Present: I bîte, sie bîtes, uie bîte, þey bîte
- Simple Past: I bit, sie bit, uie bit, þey bit
- Past Participle: (have) bitan

Key: All return to /i/ vowel in participle with -an ending, but past tense varies between /o/ (drove, rose) and short /i/ (bit, hid).

PATTERN C: A/E → Ô → A/E ABLAUT
--------------------------------
Verbs with /a/ or /e/ shifting to /o/ in past

Subpattern C1 - with -cue ending:
tâcue (tôc/tâcan), seacue (seôc/seacan), forsâcue (forsôc/forsâcan)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to seacue
- Simple Present: I seac, sie seacs, uie seacue, þey seacue, you seac/seacue
- Simple Past: I seôc, sie seôc, uie seôc, þey seôc, you seôc
- Past Participle: (have) seacan

Subpattern C2 - without -cue:
breic(ue (breoc/breocan), spique(spiec (spoc/spocan), uâc(ue (uoc, uocan), stiale (stole/stolen), friese (frose/frosen)

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to stiale
- Simple Present: I stiale, sie stiales, uie stiale, þey stiale
- Simple Past: I stole, sie stole, uie stole, þey stole
- Past Participle: (have) stolen

Key: The -cue verbs show vowel change â/ea → ô/eo, while non-cue verbs show different vowel patterns (i → o, ia → o). Most participles end in -an/-en.

PATTERN D: -EIRE → -ORE → -ORN ABLAUT
-------------------------------
Verbs showing /e/ or /a/ to /o/ with -orn participles: beire, teire, sueire, ueire

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to beire
- Simple Present: I beire, sie beires, uie beire, þey beire
- Simple Past: I bore, sie bore, uie bore, þey bore
- Past Participle: (have) born

Key: Past participles consistently take -orn ending.

PATTERN E: O → E → O ABLAUT
----------------------------
Verbs with /o/ alternating to /e/: þroe, groe, bloe, gnoe, seoe, soe

Conjugation:
- Infinitive: to groe
- Simple Present: I groe, sie groes, uie groe, þey groe
- Simple Past: I grou, sie grou, uie grou, þey grou
- Past Participle: (have) grone

Key: Past forms show /ou/ diphthong; participles take -one ending.

PATTERN F: SUPPLETIVE VERBS
----------------------------
Verbs with completely different stems: goe (uaint/gaon), be (uas/uere/bign), do (did/don)

Conjugation:
- to goe: I goe, sie goes → I uaint → (have) gaon
- to be: I am, sie is, þey are → I uas, þey uere → (have) bign
- to do: I dou, sie dus → I did → (have) don

Key: These preserve ancient Indo-European root alternations.

AUXILIARY PATTERNS
------------------
Modal and auxiliary verbs with unique behaviors: can/could, uill/uoûd, have/had

These lack participles and show tense through stem change rather than endings.

REGULAR SUFFIXES
----------------
All patterns use consistent suffixes:
- 3rd person singular present: -s (drincs, drîves, beires)
- Present participle: -ing (drinking, drîving, beiring)
- Negative contractions: standard (can't, uon't, didn't)

ETYMOLOGY NOTE
--------------
These verbs preserve the Germanic strong verb system, organizing into seven historical classes based on root vowel.
The three-part ablaut system (present-past-participle) maintains distinct forms for each principal part.
The -cue suffix appears in verbs from Old English Class III strong verbs with nasal consonants.
