Definite and Indefinite Articles in Inglish

Inglisce uses an article system inspired by Romance languages, with special consideration for phonetic harmony. 
Articles change form based on the stress pattern of the word.

Definite Articles

1. þe - Used with nouns beginning with consonants whose stress is on the first syllable. These words are "masculine"
2. þa - Used with beginning with consonants whose stress is NOT on the first syllable. These words are "feminine"
3. þi - Used with ALL nouns beginning with vowels (overrides stress consideration). These words are either grammatical gender - depending on the stress.

Feminine Nouns

    Words where the stress is not on the first syllable:
        þa contâgion(s)
        þa criâcion(s)
        þa hipnôsis, hipnôsies
        þa paràlisis, paràlisies
        þa relâcion(s)
        þa universetie, universetis
        
    Irregulars:
        þa fimele(s)
        
    Nouns ending in -abilatie are feminine:
        þa câpabilatie


Words Starting with a Vowel

    Use "þi" regardless of gender.
        þi oare, oars (Modern English "hour") (masc)
        þi animal(s) (masc)
        þi apple, appels (masc)
        þi anàlysis, anàlysies (fem)
        þi ombrella(s) (fem)
        þi ambicion(s) (fem)
        þi aplicâcion(s) (fem)
        þi ox, þi oxen (masc)
        þi oven(s) (masc)
        þi ax(es) (masc)


Indefinite Articles

The indefinite articles follow easy phonetic rules:

"a" - comes before consonant sounds and ú/eu. Examples: a dog, a haûse, a únicorn, a europían citie

"an" - comes before vowels (except ú/eu). Examples: an egg, an ombrella, an ónest person, an oare


Articles with Adjectives

When an adjective comes between the definite article and noun:
- If the adjective starts with a vowel, use "þi" for phonetic harmony
- Example: þe big boie, þe small church, BUT þi ôld church
- Indefinite articles follow phonetic harmony: 'a' before consonants or ú/eu, 'an' before vowels.


Summary

Masculine Nouns þe:
- Words with stress on the first syllable
- Words ending in -age
- Examples: þe boie, þe mâle, þe tîme, þe village

Feminine Nouns þa:
- Words with stress NOT on the first syllable
- Words ending in -abilatie
- Examples: þa relâcion, þa universetie

Rules for þi:
- ANY noun starting with a vowel sound uses "þi", regardless of gender
- Examples: þi animal (masc.), þi ombrella (fem.), þi oare
